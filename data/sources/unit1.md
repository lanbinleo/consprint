1.1 - Interaction of Heredity and Environment
Heredity
oThe genetic transfer of characteristics from parents to offspring. 
o孩子继承了父母的高个子基因
Nature
oThe influence of our inherited characteristics on our personality and physical growth. 
o一个人天生就比较内向
o有一些看到就应该定位是bio/nature的词：temperament / diathesis / mature …（还有啥？）
Nurture
oThe influence of environmental factors on a person. 
o后天培养和家庭环境影响了孩子的成就
Genetic predisposition
oAn increased likelihood of developing a particular disease based on a person’s genetic makeup. 
o家族中有抑郁症史，个体更有可能表现出抑郁倾向
Evolutionary perspective
oStudy of the evolution of behavior and the mind, using principles of natural selection. 
o从进化角度解释为什么人类普遍害怕蛇和蜘蛛
oSurvival & reproduction
Natural selection
oTraits leading to increased reproduction and survival will be passed on to succeeding generations. 
o跑得快的远古人类更有可能生存并传下基因
Eugenics
oThe study of agency under social control that may improve or impair the racial qualities of future generations.
o历史上通过筛选所谓优良基因来改善人口质量的极端主张
oSocial Darwinism → 是不对的！
oEu- “good” 记得eustress吗？
Twin studies
oResearch comparing identical twins and fraternal twins to determine the relative importance of heredity and environment. 
o观察分开抚养的双胞胎在智力上的相似性
oLongitudinal study / correlation / case study，因为ethics很难真的做experiment
Family studies
oResearch examining the tendency of traits to run in families. 
o研究近亲之间患某种精神疾病的概率
Adoption studies
oResearch comparing adopted children to their biological and adoptive parents. 
o发现孩子的性格更像亲生父母而非养父母
1.2 - Overview of the Nervous System
Central nervous system
oComposed of the brain and spinal cord. 
o作为身体的指挥中心处理所有信息
o不会还有同学记不住nervous system的分类吧！不会吧！
Peripheral nervous system
oThe sensory and motor neurons that connect the CNS to the rest of the body. 
o将手指触碰火苗的感觉传回神经中枢
Autonomic nervous system
oPart of the PNS that controls the glands and muscles of internal organs. 
o自动调节心跳和消化，不需要大脑刻意去想
o 包括sympathetic / parasympathetic
Sympathetic nervous system
oArouses the body, mobilizing its energy in stressful situations. 
o遇到老虎时，它让你瞳孔放大、心跳加快、胃部不工作
oArousal → Hormones: adrenaline / cortisol
Parasympathetic nervous system
oCalms the body, conserving its energy. 
o危机过后，它让身体恢复平静和节能状态
oCalm and digest 考完SAT才感觉有点饿了
Somatic nervous system
oControls the body’s skeletal muscles. 
o你想挥手打招呼，它控制手臂肌肉移动
oVoluntary movement
o和soma- 相关，就是身体的意思：somatosensory cortex (tactile sense); soma of neuron
1.3 - The Neuron and Neural Firing
Neurons
oBasic building blocks of the nervous system. 
o大脑中负责传递信息的神经细胞
Glial cells
oCells in the nervous system that support, nourish, and protect neurons. 
o为神经元提供营养的后勤细胞
Reflex arc
oA neural pathway that controls a reflex. 
o手碰到热锅立刻缩回来，信息没到大脑就在脊髓完成了回路
o和developmental psych 的那个rooting reflex不一样，rooting只有婴儿有，但是reflex arc是啥时候都存在，理论上路易十六也会有（目移）
Sensory neurons
oCarry incoming information from the sensory receptors to the CNS. 
o把眼睛看到的光信号传给大脑
Motor neurons
oCarry outgoing information from the CNS to muscles and glands. 
o大脑发出指令让腿部肌肉跑步
Interneurons
oNeurons within the CNS that communicate internally and intervene between sensory and motor outputs. 
o在脊髓中连接感觉神经元和运动神经元的中间人
Neural transmission
oThe process by which signaling molecules are released by a neuron and bind to and activate the receptors of another neuron. 
o电信号在神经元之间转化和传递的过程
Action potential
oA brief electrical charge that travels down an axon. 
o神经冲动像闪电一样沿轴突传导
All-or-nothing principle
oA neuron’s reaction of either firing or not firing. 
o就像开枪，扣动扳机要么发射，要么不发射，没有半发子弹
Depolarization
oThe process during the action potential when sodium is rushing into the cell. 
o细胞膜内外电荷反转，由负变正
o在做题的时候，depolarization就意味着action potential / neural impulse
Refractory period
oA period of inactivity after a neuron has fired. 
o神经元发射后的短暂冷却期，此时无法再次发射
o分成absolute / relative (但是确实没见过题考过这么细)
Resting potential
oThe positive-outside/negative-inside state of a neuron’s membrane. 
o神经元在没干活时的静息状态
Reuptake
oA neurotransmitter’s reabsorption by the sending neuron. 
o神经递质完成任务后被重新回收利用
oSSRI → MDD
Threshold
oThe level of stimulation required to trigger a neural impulse. 
o刺激必须达到一定强度才能让神经元放电
Multiple sclerosis
oA disease in which the immune system eats away at the protective covering of nerves. 
o髓鞘受损导致神经信号传递变慢或中断
oMS (myelin sheath)→ MS
Myasthenia gravis
oA chronic autoimmune disease that affects the neuromuscular junction and produces serious weakness of voluntary muscles. 
o表现为眼睑下垂或严重的肌肉无力
oACh
Dopamine
oNeurotransmitter associated with movement, learning, attention, and emotion. 
o中彩票时的奖赏感和愉悦感
o和addiction有关，比如说上瘾玩游戏，是因为dopamine的奖励机制
o(+) schizophrenia (只是correlation) (-) Parkinson’s disease
Excitatory neurotransmitters
oIncrease the likelihood that the neuron will fire an action potential. 
o给神经递质发出的加油指令，速速传递消息！
Inhibitory neurotransmitters
oDecrease the likelihood that the neuron will fire an action potential. 
o给神经递质发出的刹车指令 
Serotonin
oAffects mood, hunger, sleep, and arousal. 
o血清素不足可能导致抑郁情绪
o所以SSRI的本质是通过抑制serotonin的reuptake → 更多serotonin传递信息
Norepinephrine
oHelps control alertness and arousal. 
o在压力下保持警觉和兴奋
Glutamate
oA major excitatory neurotransmitter involved in memory. 
o帮助你更好地记住单词
GABA
oA major inhibitory neurotransmitter. 
o帮助大脑冷静下来，减少焦虑
oAlcohol是GABA agonist → 让你calm down → impulse control↓ → 会放松 / 嗜睡 / 反应变慢（alcohol作用的主要地点是prefrontal cortex + cerebellum）
Endorphins
oNatural, opiate-like neurotransmitters linked to pain control and pleasure. 
o长跑后的快感，减轻身体的疼痛
oPainkiller → 所以可以说是一种negative reinforcement
Substance P
oA neurotransmitter involved in pain perception. 
o手指扎破时向大脑发送疼痛信号
Acetylcholine (ACh)
oEnables muscle action, learning, and memory. 
o控制肌肉收缩，缺失与阿尔兹海默症有关
oMG 肌无力！
Hormones
oChemical messengers that are manufactured by the endocrine glands. 
o通过血液运输的长效化学信息
o本质和neurotransmitter是一样的，只是作用的地点不一致
Adrenaline
oHormone secreted in response to stress, increasing rates of blood circulation and breathing. 
o被Irene提问的时候你心跳狂飙
Leptin
oHormone that helps regulate energy balance by inhibiting hunger. 
o告诉大脑你吃饱了 → 传递full signal
o这俩和satiety相关的hormones在UNIT 4 Motivation~
Ghrelin
oHormone secreted by empty stomach that sends “I’m hungry" signals to the brain. 
o肚子饿得咕咕叫时，它催你快去吃饭 “咕”relin
Melatonin
oHormone that helps regulate sleep-wake cycles. 
o天黑时分泌增多，让你产生睡意
o和光线有关：睡前看手机，蓝光太多大脑就不会分泌melatonin
Oxytocin
oHormone associated with social bonding and trust. 
o拥抱时分泌的爱与信任的荷尔蒙
Psychoactive drugs
oChemicals that affect the central nervous system and alter activity in the brain. 
o改变情绪和认知的化学物质
Agonists
oDrugs that increase the action of a neurotransmitter. 
o模仿神经递质的作用 Mimicry & activation
Antagonists
oDrugs that block the function of a neurotransmitter. 
o阻断受体，让神经递质无法工作 Block & inhibition 
Reuptake inhibitors
oDrugs that prevent the reabsorption of neurotransmitters. 
o让神经递质在间隙停留更久，从而增强效果
oAgain! SSRI
Stimulants
oDrugs that excite neural activity and speed up body functions. 
o让你精神百倍的物质
o咖啡！上课喝咖啡，那考试前也喝，想起来的知识点可能更多，这叫什么呢？—— State dependent memory (Unit 2)
Caffeine
oA mild stimulant found in coffee and tea. 
o早起喝一杯咖啡来提神
Cocaine
oA powerful and addictive stimulant. 
o导致极度兴奋并具有极强成瘾性
oIndirect agonist → Cocaine inhibits the reuptake of dopamine
Depressants
oDrugs that reduce neural activity and slow body functions. 让神经系统变迟钝
Alcohol
oThe most common depressant. 
o喝醉后反应迟钝、走路不稳
Hallucinogens
oPsychedelic drugs that distort perceptions and evoke sensory images in the absence of sensory input. 
o看到并不存在的幻象
o所以看到halluci- 就是和distorted perception相关 
Marijuana
oA drug that causes mild hallucinations and relaxation. 
o吸食后感到放松且感知力改变
Opioids
oDrugs used to treat pain but are highly addictive. 
o强效止痛药，如吗啡
Heroin
oAn extremely addictive opioid drug. 
o极难戒掉的毒品
Tolerance
oDiminishing effect with regular use of the same dose of a drug. 
o以前喝一杯瑞幸就清醒，现在要喝三杯才有同样的效果
Addiction
oCompulsive drug craving and use, despite adverse consequences. 
o无法停止吸烟，即便知道伤害身体
Withdrawal
oDiscomfort and distress that follow discontinuing an addictive drug. 
o戒烟后的焦虑和渴望感

1.4 - The Brain
Brain stem
oThe oldest part and central core of the brain, responsible for automatic survival functions. 
o维持生命的底线 Life-sustaining functions
Medulla
oBase of the brainstem; controls heartbeat and breathing. 
o如果这里受损，人就无法呼吸or心跳
Reticular activating system (RAS)
oNerve network in the brainstem that plays an important role in controlling arousal. 
o负责你的觉醒和清醒状态
Reward center
oRegion of the brain that produces feelings of pleasure. 
o大脑中的dopamine通路，让人想重复愉悦行为
Cerebellum
oThe “little brain” at the rear of the brainstem; coordinates voluntary movement and balance. 
o让你能平稳地走平衡木 Balance & coordination & posture
Cerebral cortex
oThe intricate fabric of interconnected neural cells covering the cerebral hemispheres. 
o大脑表层那层褶皱，负责高级认知
Limbic system
oNeural system associated with emotions and drives. 
o大脑的情感中心
Thalamus
oThe brain’s sensory control center. 
o除了olfaction，所有的感觉信息都要经过这个relay station，再到各个感觉处理皮层
Hypothalamus
oDirects eating, drinking, body temperature; helps govern the endocrine system. 
o维持身体的内环境稳态 homeostasis
Pituitary gland
oThe endocrine system’s most influential gland. 
o主宰其他腺体的总司令 master gland
Hippocampus
oA neural center located in the limbic system that helps process explicit memories for storage.
o如果这里坏了，你就记不住psych的知识点辣！
oMemory formation & navigation 
oH.M. anterograde amnesia也是因为hippocampus坏了 （但还有implicit memory）
Amygdala
oTwo lima-bean-sized neural clusters in the limbic system; linked to emotion, especially fear and aggression. 
o看到蛇时产生的恐惧反应 “fear center”
Corpus callosum
oThe large band of neural fibers connecting the two brain hemispheres. 
o左右脑沟通的桥梁
oEpilepsy → 之前的治疗方式是切CC → split brain → 知道了brain lateralization
Occipital lobes
oPortion of the cerebral cortex lying at the back of the head; includes areas that receive information from the visual fields. 
o如果你后脑勺挨一记闷棍，可能会眼冒金星
o（O. O  ）像不像你的眼睛 ∴o lobe → vision
Temporal lobes
oPortion of the cerebral cortex lying roughly above the ears; includes the auditory areas. 
o负责处理听到的声音
Parietal lobes
oPortion of the cerebral cortex lying at the top of the head; receives sensory input for touch and body position. 
o感知冷热和触碰的部位 somatosensory cortex在这里
Association areas
oAreas of the cerebral cortex that are involved in higher mental functions such as learning, remembering, thinking, and speaking. 
o大脑中不直接负责感官或运动，而是负责深度加工的区域
Somatosensory cortex
oArea at the front of the parietal lobes that registers and processes body touch and movement sensations. 
oLeo脚趾被Jason踩了，大脑在这个区域产生痛觉感应
Frontal lobes
oInvolved in speaking and muscle movements and in making plans and judgments. 
o大脑的理智中心
Linguistic processing
oThe process of understanding and using language. 
o大脑对文字和语音的解析  左脑！
Higher-order thinking
oComplex cognitive processes including problem solving and decision making. 
o思考量子力学等抽象问题
Executive functioning
oHigh-level cognitive processes that help individuals manage goals and control behaviors. 
o在诱惑面前控制住自己不去玩手机
Prefrontal cortex
oResponsible for thinking, planning, and language. 
o前额叶受损可能导致性格大变，变得冲动易怒
Motor cortex
oAn area at the rear of the frontal lobes that controls voluntary movements. 
o大脑发令让手指打字写FRQ的源头
Split brain research
oStudies on people whose corpus callosum has been severed. 
o揭示了左右脑分工的秘密
Hemispheric specialization
oThe idea that certain cognitive functions are localized in one hemisphere or the other. 
o左脑侧重逻辑和语言，右脑侧重空间感和art
Broca’s area
oControls language expression; an area in the frontal lobe that directs the muscle movements involved in speech. 
o受损后你能听懂话，但无法流利表达
Wernicke’s area
oControls language reception; an area in the temporal lobe involved in language comprehension.
o受损后你说话很顺溜但满嘴胡言，且听不懂别人的话
Aphasia
oImpairment of language, usually caused by left hemisphere damage either to Broca’s area or to Wernicke’s area. 
o脑卒中后出现的言语障碍 有以上两个脑区的aphasia
Contralateral hemispheric organization
oThe left hemisphere controls the right side of the body, and vice versa. 
o右手被扎时，左脑process疼的新号
Plasticity
oThe brain’s ability to change, especially during childhood, by reorganizing after damage or by building new pathways based on experience. 
o盲人的听觉区域可能会接管部分视觉区域的功能
o越年轻越有plasticity (或者有的时候就叫neuroplasticity)
EEG
oAn amplified recording of the waves of electrical activity sweeping across the brain’s surface. 
o医生在头上贴电极来观察你的脑电波
o不需要记全名 考试中会给简写
fMRI
oA technique for revealing blood flow and, therefore, brain activity by comparing successive MRI scans. 
o通过观察大脑哪里在耗氧来判断你在想什么
o和EEG一样都是去测brain activity的，但是一个是通过brainwave，一个是血氧
Lesioning
oTissue destruction. 
o通过损毁特定大脑区域来观察其功能的丧失
o最开始的时候只能通过这些lesioning case study来研究大脑
1.5 - Sleep
Consciousness
oOur awareness of ourselves and our environment. 
o你现在能意识到你在看这段文字，希望你Psych 5分~
Circadian rhythm
oThe biological clock; regular bodily rhythms that occur on a 24-hour cycle. 
o你的体温和睡眠呈现24小时规律波动 是一天的变化
Jet lag
oFatigue and sleep disturbance resulting from disruption of the body’s normal circadian rhythm. 
o从北京飞往纽约后感到黑白颠倒
Shift work
oWork scheduled outside of standard daytime hours. 
o你熬夜复习AP导致睡眠规律被打乱 (但这个应该叫shift study?)
NREM stage 1
oThe transition from wakefulness to sleep. 
o刚入睡时半梦半醒的状态
Hypnagogic sensations
oBizarre experiences, such as jerking or a feeling of falling, while transitioning to sleep. 
o睡觉时突然感觉身体踩空蹬了一下or 听到不存在的声音，看到闪光等
NREM stage 2
oDeeper sleep characterized by sleep spindles. 
o睡眠中占比例最大、包含sleep spindle的阶段
NREM stage 3
oDeepest stage of sleep (delta waves)
o熟睡期，很难被叫醒，可能出现somnambulism
REM sleep
oRapid eye movement sleep; a recurring sleep stage during which vivid dreams commonly occur.
o大脑极度活跃但身体瘫痪，正在做梦
oActive brainwaves (而且心跳呼吸会加快) + muscle paralysis
REM rebound
oThe tendency for REM sleep to increase following REM sleep deprivation. 
o熬夜后补觉，梦会变得特别多
Activation-synthesis theory
oThe theory that dreams are the brain’s attempt to synthesize random neural activity. 
o梦是大脑在整理随机神经电信号
o所以这个理论在说dreams是没有意义的，是random by-product, 但是psychodynamics觉得是unconsciousness的体现，所以超级有意义，要interpretation of dreams
Consolidation theory
oDreams help us sort out the day’s events and consolidate our memories. 
o做梦是在帮我们把记忆存进LTM
Restoration of resources
oSleep helps restore our bodies and immune systems. 
o睡觉是为了恢复体力 特别是NREM阶段
Insomnia
oRecurring problems in falling or staying asleep. 
o躺在床上翻来覆去睡不着
Narcolepsy
oA sleep disorder characterized by uncontrollable sleep attacks. 
o说着说着话突然秒睡过去 可能是在情感释放之后突然发作
REM sleep behavior disorder
oA sleep disorder in which you physically act out vivid, often unpleasant dreams. 
o在梦里打架，结果现实中也挥舞拳头踢人
Sleep apnea
oA sleep disorder characterized by temporary cessations of breathing during sleep. 
o打呼噜时突然呼吸停了几秒
Somnambulism

1.6 - Sensation
Sensation
oThe process by which our sensory receptors and nervous system receive and represent stimulus energies from our environment. 
o鼻子闻到了花香
Transduction
oConversion of one form of energy into another. 
o耳朵将声波信号转化成神经电脉冲
o是sensation的一部分
Absolute threshold
oThe minimum stimulation needed to detect a particular stimulus 50% of the time. 
o你在黑夜中能看到的最微弱的一丝光
o是sense or not sense的那个界限
Just-noticeable difference (JND)
oThe minimum difference between two stimuli required for detection 50% of the time. 
o两袋糖要相差多少克你才能感觉出一袋比另一袋重
o基数大，需要感受到差别的区间就更大
Weber’s law
oTo be perceived as different, two stimuli must differ by a constant minimum percentage. 
o比起总量的绝对差，我们更在意变化的百分比
Sensory adaptation
oDiminished sensitivity as a consequence of constant stimulation. 
o刚进游泳池觉得冷，过一会儿就适应了水温
o一般来说题目都在考olfaction的sensory adaptation
Sensory interaction
oThe principle that one sense may influence another. 
o感冒鼻塞时，吃东西会觉得没味道
Synesthesia
oDescribing one kind of sensation in terms of another. 
o听到某种音乐时会看到特定的颜色
o这是一种非常rare condition 不是每个人都有
Retina
oThe light-sensitive inner surface of the eye. 
o眼睛里的底片，负责捕捉影像
o里面有rods and cones的photoreceptors
Blind spot
oThe point at which the optic nerve leaves the eye, creating a “blind” spot because no receptor cells are located there. 
o光线射到视神经出口处时，我们看不见东西
o在这个点上，没有任何的photoreceptor，但是大脑有top-down processing，所以会自动补全缺失的那一小点视觉信息
Visual nerve
oThe nerve that carries neural impulses from the eye to the brain. 
o把视觉信号传送到大脑的电缆
Lens
oThe transparent structure behind the pupil that changes shape to help focus images on the retina. 
o眼睛里的变焦镜头 Accommodation
Accommodation
oThe process by which the eye’s lens changes shape to focus near or far objects on the retina. 
o看远处的山再看手中的书，晶状体在自动对焦
o这个词在AP Psych有两个含义，别忘了Piaget
Nearsightedness
oCondition where far objects are blurry. 
o不带眼镜看不清黑板
Farsightedness
oCondition where near objects are blurry. 
o老人家看报纸要拿得很远
Photoreceptors
oCells in the retina that respond to light. 
o感光细胞，分为视杆和视锥
Rods
oRetinal receptors that detect black, white, and gray; necessary for peripheral and twilight vision.
o在昏暗环境下帮你走路不撞墙
oPeripheral vision，所以fovea有很多cones分辨颜色，但是旁边的视线就很难分辨颜色
Cones
oRetinal receptor cells that are concentrated near the center of the retina and that function in daylight or in well-lit conditions. 
o帮你分辨CB上不同subject的颜色 (我其实不是很喜欢psych的这种粉色)
oCones → Colors
Trichromatic theory
oThe theory that the retina contains three different color receptors—one most sensitive to red, one to green, one to blue. 
o认为我们只有RGB三种像素点
Opponent-process theory
oThe theory that opposing retinal processes (red-green, yellow-blue, white-black) enable color vision. 
o颜色是以对立组合的方式处理的
o要记得颜色的组合，会考！
Afterimages
oImages that occur when a visual sensation persists for a brief time after the original stimulus is removed. 
o盯着红色看久了，看白墙时会出现绿色的影子
o是opponent-process theory的体现
Ganglion cells
oThe specialized cells which lie behind the bipolar cells whose axons form the optic nerve. 
o将初步处理后的视觉信号汇总传往大脑
Dichromatism
oA condition in which a person has only two types of cone cells. 
o红绿色盲
Monochromatism
oA condition in which a person has only one type of cone cell and can only see in shades of gray.
o全色盲，世界只有黑白灰
o普通人是tri，只有两种cones叫di，那mono就是全色盲
Prosopagnosia
oFace blindness. 
o能看清五官但认不出这人是谁
o它是top-down processing出现了问题
Blindsight
oA condition in which a person can respond to a visual stimulus without consciously experiencing it. 
o失明者能避开地上的障碍物 neuroplasticity 
Wavelength
oThe distance from the peak of one light or sound wave to the peak of the next. 
o决定了光的hue或声音的pitch
o（在这个词里找到color了吗
Pitch
oA tone’s experienced highness or lowness; depends on frequency. 
o女高音的高音
Amplitude
oThe height of a wave. 
o决定了光的brightness或声音的loudness
Loudness
oA sensory characteristic of sound produced by the amplitude (intensity) of the sound wave. 
oJustin上课时候的超大音量
Pitch perception
oHow we hear the frequency of sound waves. 
o大脑对高低音的分辨过程
Place theory
oThe theory that links the pitch we hear with the place where the cochlea’s membrane is stimulated. 
o高频音刺激耳蜗的底部
o和frequency theory 一起解释了为什么我们会听到不同的pitch
Frequency theory
oThe rate of nerve impulses traveling up the auditory nerve matches the frequency of a tone. 
o低频音让神经纤维以同样的频率震动
Volley theory
oGroups of neurons fire in rapid succession to achieve higher combined frequencies. 
o中频音通过神经元轮班放电来感知
Sound localization
oDetermining the location of a sound source. 
o根据声音到达左右耳的微小时间差判断声源
Conduction deafness
oHearing loss caused by damage to the mechanical system that conducts sound waves to the cochlea. 
o鼓膜破裂导致的听力下降
o相对来说还是比较好介入处理的，因为神经还好着
Sensorineural deafness
oHearing loss caused by damage to the cochlea’s receptor cells or to the auditory nerves. 
o长期听重金属摇滚导致毛细胞受损
o不需要记cochlea (耳蜗)，但需要记得是和神经相关的一种耳聋 → 人工耳蜗
Olfactory system
oThe sensory system used for smelling. 
o人类的嗅觉系统 不通过thalamus!
Pheromones
oChemical signals released by an animal that communicate information and affect the behavior of other animals of the same species. 
o蚂蚁留下的气味路径 动物有的信息素
Gustation
oThe sense of taste.
o舌头上的味觉
Sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami, oleogustus
oThe six basic tastes, which refer to the tastes of sugar, acid, salt, bitter substances, savory flavor, and fat respectively.
o辣不是味觉
Taste receptors
oCells on the tongue that respond to sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami, and oleogustus. 
o味蕾
Supertasters
oPeople who have more taste buds than the average person. 
o对味道极度敏感，通常讨厌吃苦味食物
Nontasters
oPeople with a low sensitivity to taste. 
o口味重，觉得大部分菜都没味道
Gate control theory
oThe theory that the spinal cord contains a neurological “gate” that blocks pain signals or allows them to pass on to the brain. 
o当你撞到腿时下意识揉一揉，可以减轻痛感
Phantom limb
oSensation that an amputated or missing limb is still attached. 
o失去手臂的人感觉断肢处在发痒
Vestibular sense
oThe sense of body movement and position, including the sense of balance. 
o让你闭着眼也能感受到自己在旋转
o知道你的头和身体的相对位置，是站着还是倒立；也知道动作，比如说车在加速前进
Semicircular canals
oThree canals within the inner ear that contain specialized receptor cells that generate nerve impulses with body movement. 
o耳蜗上方的管子，像水平仪一样维持平衡，里面有fluid
Kinesthesis
oThe system for sensing the position and movement of individual body parts. 
o让你闭着眼也能准确摸到自己的鼻子
