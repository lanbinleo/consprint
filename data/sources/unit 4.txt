4.1 - Attribution Theory and Person Perception

   - Attributions
      - How we explain the causes of events or behaviors — either internal or external.
      - 我们怎么解释一件事发生的原因，比如他为什么迟到

   - Dispositional attributions
      - Explaining behavior as caused by <red>internal</red> factors like personality, ability, or effort.
      - 他考试考得好，是因为他聪明

   - Situational attributions
      - Explaining behavior as caused by <red>external</red> factors like luck, context, or difficulty.
      - 他考试考得好，是因为这次题目简单

   - Explanatory style
      - A person's habitual way of explaining events, either optimistically or pessimistically.
      - 一个人习惯性地怎么解释发生在自己身上的事

   - Optimistic explanatory style
      - Bad events are seen as **temporary, external, and specific**.
      - 考试考砸了：这次题太偏了，下次不会这样

   - Pessimistic explanatory style
      - Bad events are seen as **permanent, internal, and global**.
      - 考试考砸了：我就是笨，什么都学不好

   - Actor/observer bias
      - When we are the **actor**, we blame the situation; when we are the **observer**, we blame the person's character. It highlights the <red>double standard</red> between judging self vs. others.
      - 自己迟到是因为堵车，别人迟到是因为没有时间观念

   - Fundamental attribution error
      - The tendency to <red>overestimate dispositional factors</red> and underestimate situational factors **when judging others**.
      - 看到别人考试挂了，觉得他就是不努力，而不考虑他可能生病了

   - Self-serving bias
      - Attributing **success to internal** factors and **failure to external** factors to protect self-esteem.
      - AP考了5分：我是天才；考了2分：题出得太偏了

   - Internal locus of control
      - Belief that you **control your own outcomes** through effort and decisions.
      - 考前认真刷题，相信努力能改变结果

   - External locus of control
      - Belief that outcomes are determined by **luck, fate, or others**. Can lead to *learned helplessness*.
      - 考前转发锦鲤，觉得考多少分是命

   - Mere exposure effect
      - Simply being exposed to something repeatedly increases our <red>liking</red> for it.
      - 一首歌刚听觉得一般，循环十遍后觉得真上头；广告反复轰炸也是这个原理

   - Self-fulfilling prophecy
      - A belief leads to behavior that makes the belief come true: **belief → behavior → outcome**.
      - 老师觉得Leo是好学生，给他更多关注，Leo更努力，最终真的成了好学生

   - Social comparison
      - Evaluating ourselves by comparing with others.
      - 看看身边同学考了多少分来判断自己考得好不好

   - Upward social comparison
      - Comparing with someone **better** — can motivate or cause feelings of inadequacy.
      - 和年级第一比，觉得自己还要更努力，或者觉得自卑

   - Downward social comparison
      - Comparing with someone **worse** — boosts self-esteem but may reduce motivation.
      - 和倒数第一比，觉得自己还不错嘛

   - Relative deprivation
      - Feeling deprived or dissatisfied **after comparing** with others who have more.
      - 本来对工资很满意，知道同事比自己多两千后就不开心了



4.2 - Attitude Formation and Attitude Change

   - Stereotype
      - An **overgeneralized belief** about a group of people; a cognitive shortcut (heuristic). Not necessarily negative but ignores individual differences.
      - 亚洲人都擅长数学，法国人都很浪漫

   - Prejudice
      - An <red>unjustified negative attitude</red> toward a group — this is the **emotional** level.
      - 我就是不喜欢吃榴莲的人

   - Discrimination
      - <red>Unjust behavior</red> toward a group — this is the **action** level, often caused by prejudice.
      - 因为不喜欢吃榴莲的人，所以不让他们加入我的小组

   - Implicit attitudes
      - Attitudes that are **unconscious** and automatically activated; people may not be aware they hold them.
      - 嘴上说不歧视，但在无意识的测试中表现出对某群体的偏见（如IAT内隐联想测试）

   - Ethnocentrism
      - Believing your own culture is **superior** and judging other cultures by your own standards.
      - 看到别人用手吃饭就觉得脏、落后，而不是理解这是不同的文化习俗

   - Just-world phenomenon
      - The belief that people **get what they deserve**, which often leads to <red>blaming the victim</red>.
      - 看到路边的乞丐就想：他肯定是年轻时不努力才沦落到这样

   - Out-group homogeneity bias
      - Perceiving members of an **out-group as all alike**, while seeing your own group as diverse.
      - 你能分清重金属、朋克、独立摇滚的区别，但觉得K-pop的歌听起来全是一个调子

   - In-group bias
      - Favoring members of your **own group** over out-group members.
      - 平时吐槽自己学校，但跟外校比赛时觉得我校最棒

   - Belief perseverance
      - Clinging to initial beliefs <red>even after they have been discredited</red> by evidence.
      - 阴谋论者看到科学证据证明地球是圆的，说这都是伪造的，地球就是平的

   - Confirmation bias
      - Seeking information that **supports** existing beliefs and ignoring contradictory evidence.
      - 觉得每次洗车后都下雨，是因为只记住了洗车后下雨的次数，忘了没下雨的

   - Cognitive dissonance
      - The psychological <red>discomfort</red> when attitudes and behaviors are **inconsistent**. To reduce it, we change either the attitude or the behavior.
      - 知道抽烟有害还继续抽，于是说服自己：抽烟能减压嘛。吃不到葡萄说葡萄酸

   - Cognitive load
      - The amount of **mental effort** being used in working memory. High cognitive load makes it harder to think critically and easier to rely on heuristics.
      - 一边开车一边打电话，大脑超负荷，容易出错



4.3 - Psychology of Social Situations

   - Social norms
      - Unwritten rules about how to behave in a group or society.
      - 在图书馆要安静，电梯里面朝门站

   - Social influence theory
      - The theory that our behavior is influenced by others through **normative** or **informational** pathways.
      - 我们的行为受到身边人的影响，不管是为了合群还是为了获取信息

   - Normative social influence
      - Conforming to be <red>accepted</red> by the group, even if you know they're wrong.
      - 一群朋友都抽烟，你明知不好但为了合群也跟着抽

   - Informational social influence
      - Conforming because you believe others have <red>better information</red> than you.
      - 第一次吃西餐不知道怎么用刀叉，偷看隔壁桌怎么做

   - Persuasion
      - The process of changing someone's attitudes or behaviors through communication.
      - 广告、演讲、谈判中试图改变你的想法

   - Elaboration likelihood model
      - A model explaining two routes of persuasion: **central** (logic) and **peripheral** (surface cues).
      - 我们被说服有两条路径：走脑子或走感觉

   - Central route
      - Persuasion through <red>logic, evidence, and careful thinking</red>. Leads to **lasting** attitude change.
      - 买电脑时仔细对比CPU、内存和价格

   - Peripheral route
      - Persuasion through <red>surface cues</red> like attractiveness or celebrity endorsement. Effect is **temporary**.
      - 我家哥哥代言联想电脑，买！

   - Halo effect
      - The tendency to let one positive trait (often **attractiveness**) influence overall judgment of a person.
      - 长得好看的人，别人会觉得他性格也好、能力也强

   - Foot-in-the-door technique
      - Getting someone to agree to a **small request first**, then following with a larger one.
      - 先请你签名支持环保，然后问你能不能捐100块

   - Door-in-the-face technique
      - Starting with a **large request** that will be refused, then following with a smaller one.
      - 先问借5000买iPad，被拒后说那借100吃个饭总行吧

   - Conformity
      - Adjusting behavior or thinking to match **group standards**.
      - 电梯里所有人都面朝后站，你不自觉也转过身去

   - Obedience
      - Complying with direct commands from an <red>authority figure</red>. (Conformity = peers; Obedience = authority)
      - 警察说不许动，你就不动了

   - Individualism
      - Cultural emphasis on **personal goals, independence, and self-expression**.
      - 美国文化强调个人自由和独立思考

   - Collectivism
      - Cultural emphasis on **group harmony, duty, and interdependence**.
      - 东亚文化强调家庭和集体利益

   - Multiculturalism
      - Valuing and integrating **diverse cultural perspectives** within a society.
      - 一个社区里不同文化背景的人互相尊重、和平共处

   - Group polarization
      - Group discussion makes members' views become <red>more extreme</red> than their initial positions.
      - 一群观点相似的人讨论后，观点变得更加极端，比如越聊越激动地蛐蛐某个人

   - Groupthink
      - Desire for group harmony suppresses **dissenting opinions**, leading to poor decisions.
      - 开会时大家都觉得方案有问题，但没人敢说，结果项目失败了

   - Diffusion of responsibility
      - The more people present, the <red>less personal responsibility</red> each person feels to act.
      - 有人倒在路上，围观的人越多，每个人越觉得别人会去帮忙

   - Social loafing
      - Individuals exert **less effort** when working in a group because their contribution can't be individually measured.
      - 三个和尚没水吃，小组作业总有人摸鱼

   - Deindividuation
      - Loss of self-awareness and self-restraint in group situations due to **anonymity and arousal**.
      - 演唱会上疯狂尖叫跟唱，键盘侠在网上骂人

   - Social facilitation
      - Presence of others **improves** performance on simple/well-practiced tasks but **impairs** performance on complex/new tasks.
      - 台球高手有人看打得更准，但第一次演讲有人看反而忘词

   - False consensus effect
      - Overestimating how much others **agree** with our own beliefs or behaviors.
      - 你觉得香菜奶茶超好喝，以为大家都喜欢，买了一堆请全班结果没人喝

   - Superordinate goals
      - A shared goal that requires <red>cooperation</red> between conflicting groups, helping to reduce hostility.
      - 两个互相仇视的小队必须一起推出陷在泥里的卡车，推完关系就好了

   - Social traps
      - Individuals pursuing **short-term self-interest** that leads to **long-term collective harm**.
      - 大家都开私家车图方便，结果导致严重拥堵和雾霾

   - Industrial-organizational (I/O) psychologists
      - Psychologists who apply psychological principles to the **workplace** to improve productivity and employee well-being.
      - 公司请心理学家设计面试题，或优化办公室布局让员工更高效

   - Burnout
      - Physical and emotional **exhaustion** caused by prolonged work-related stress.
      - 连续加班几个月后感到身心俱疲，对工作完全失去热情

   - Altruism
      - Selfless concern for the well-being of others, with **no expectation of reward**.
      - 匿名捐款给灾区，不求任何回报

   - Prosocial behavior
      - Any action intended to **help others**, which may or may not be selfless.
      - 帮老奶奶过马路，扶起摔倒的小朋友

   - Social debt
      - The feeling of **owing** someone after they have helped you.
      - 朋友帮你搬家后，你觉得欠了个人情

   - Social reciprocity norm
      - The expectation that people will **return favors** — help those who have helped you.
      - 别人帮了我，我以后也要帮他

   - Social responsibility norm
      - The expectation that people should **help those in need**, even if they can't repay.
      - 帮助弱势群体，即使他们无法回报

   - Bystander effect
      - The more people present at an emergency, the <red>less likely</red> any individual is to help.
      - 大街上有人晕倒，围观的人很多但没人打120

   - Situational variables
      - External factors in the environment that influence whether someone will help.
      - 天黑、偏僻的地方，人们更不愿意伸出援手

   - Attentional variables
      - Whether a person **notices** the event and recognizes it as an emergency, affecting helping behavior.
      - 戴着耳机走路，根本没注意到有人摔倒了



4.4 - Psychodynamic and Humanistic Theories of Personality

   - Psychodynamic theory
      - Freud's approach emphasizing <red>unconscious processes, childhood experiences, and internal conflicts</red> as drivers of personality and behavior.
      - 弗洛伊德认为人的行为受潜意识驱动，童年经历影响一生

   - Unconscious processes
      - Thoughts, desires, and memories that are outside conscious awareness but still influence behavior. The deepest level of Freud's iceberg model.
      - 被压抑的童年创伤记忆，自己想不起来但影响着现在的行为

   - Ego defense mechanisms
      - Unconscious strategies the **ego** uses to reduce anxiety from conflicts between the id, superego, and reality.
      - 大脑自动启动的心理保护机制，用来缓解焦虑

   - Denial
      - Refusing to accept a painful reality.
      - 亲人去世后说：这不可能，一定是搞错了！

   - Displacement
      - Redirecting emotions toward a <red>safer or weaker target</red>.
      - 被老板骂了不敢还嘴，回家对家人撒气

   - Projection
      - Attributing your own unacceptable thoughts or feelings to **someone else**.
      - 以小人之心度君子之腹，自己自私却觉得别人都自私

   - Rationalization
      - Creating logical-sounding excuses to justify unacceptable behavior or feelings.
      - 考砸了说不是没努力，是考试太难了

   - Reaction formation
      - Behaving in a way <red>opposite</red> to one's true feelings.
      - 喜欢一个人却故意对他冷淡甚至刻薄

   - Regression
      - Reverting to **childlike** behaviors when stressed.
      - 成年人遇到压力大哭大闹要找妈妈

   - Repression
      - Unconsciously **blocking** disturbing thoughts or memories from awareness. (The most basic defense mechanism)
      - 完全想不起小时候被伤害的经历，不是故意忘而是真的记不起来

   - Sublimation
      - Channeling unacceptable impulses into **socially acceptable** or productive activities.
      - 化悲愤为力量，失恋后疯狂健身

   - Projective tests
      - Personality assessments using **ambiguous stimuli** (e.g., inkblots) to reveal unconscious thoughts. Limited in *reliability and validity*.
      - 罗夏墨迹测验：给你看一团墨迹，让你说看到了什么，以此推断潜意识

   - Preconscious mind
      - Information not currently in awareness but **easily retrievable**.
      - 你现在没在想你早餐吃了什么，但一问你马上就能想起来

   - Unconscious mind
      - The deepest level containing <red>repressed</red> thoughts, desires, and memories that are not accessible to conscious awareness.
      - 被压抑的痛苦记忆和不可接受的欲望，自己根本意识不到

   - Humanistic psychology
      - An approach emphasizing **free will, self-actualization, and inherent goodness**. Key figure: Carl Rogers.
      - 每个人都有积极向上的潜能，强调人的自由意志

   - Unconditional regard
      - Accepting and loving someone <red>without judgment or conditions</red>. (Unconditional Positive Regard)
      - 无论孩子做了什么，父母都表达接纳和爱，不附带条件

   - Self-actualizing tendency
      - The innate drive to **grow, develop, and fulfill one's potential**.
      - 人天生就有不断追求成为更好自己的内在驱动力



4.5 - Social-Cognitive and Trait Theories of Personality

   - Social-cognitive theory
      - Personality is shaped by the **interaction** of personal cognitive factors, behavior, and environment.
      - 人的性格是认知、行为和环境三者相互影响的结果

   - Reciprocal determinism
      - Bandura's concept that **personal factors, behavior, and environment** all mutually influence each other.
      - 一个被贴上坏学生标签的孩子（环境）→ 自我认知变差（认知）→ 不想学习（行为）→ 成绩更差（环境强化）→ 恶性循环

   - Self-concept
      - A person's overall perception and belief about **who they are**. (Answers "Who am I?")
      - 我是一个内向的、喜欢画画的高中生

   - Self-efficacy
      - Belief in one's ability to <red>succeed at a specific task</red>.
      - 我相信我能通过明天的AP考试

   - Self-esteem
      - One's overall <red>sense of self-worth</red>. Unlike self-efficacy, it's about general value, not task-specific.
      - 无论考好考差，我都觉得自己是有价值的人

   - Trait theories
      - Theories that describe personality as a set of **stable, measurable characteristics** (traits).
      - 用一组稳定的特质来描述人格，比如外向、尽责

   - Big Five theory
      - The most widely accepted trait model: **OCEAN** — Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism (Emotional Stability).
      - 用五个维度描述人格的模型

   - Agreeableness
      - Tendency toward trust, altruism, kindness, and cooperation. Low = competitive or manipulative.
      - 高分：主动帮助同事；低分：为达目的不择手段

   - Extraversion
      - Tendency toward sociability, talkativeness, and positive emotions. Low (introversion) = reserved, needs alone time.
      - 高分：参加派对很兴奋；低分：独处才能充电

   - Conscientiousness
      - Tendency toward being organized, dependable, and self-disciplined. Low = careless, procrastinating.
      - 高分：每天列待办清单；低分：经常忘记截止日期

   - Openness to experience
      - Tendency toward curiosity, creativity, and appreciation of new ideas. Low = conventional, prefers routine.
      - 高分：喜欢探索艺术和哲学；低分：喜欢按部就班

   - Emotional Stability
      - The opposite end of **Neuroticism**. High stability = calm, resilient; Low stability (high neuroticism) = anxious, moody.
      - 高分：泰山崩于前而色不变；低分：遇到小事就焦虑

   - Personality inventories
      - Standardized **self-report questionnaires** designed to assess personality traits.
      - MMPI、大五人格问卷，通过一系列问题来评估你的人格特质

   - Factor analysis
      - A **statistical method** used to identify clusters of correlated traits, which was used to develop the Big Five.
      - 通过统计方法把大量人格数据归纳成几个核心因素，大五人格就是这么来的



4.6 - Motivation

   - Drive-reduction theory
      - Physiological needs create a state of tension (**drive**) that motivates behavior to reduce the drive and restore <red>homeostasis</red>.
      - 口渴（需要）→ 想喝水（驱力）→ 喝水（行为）→ 不渴了（平衡恢复）

   - Homeostasis
      - The body's tendency to maintain a **stable internal state** (e.g., temperature, blood sugar).
      - 身体自动维持体温在37度左右

   - Arousal theory
      - We are motivated to maintain an <red>optimal level of arousal</red> — not too high, not too low.
      - 无聊时想找点刺激，太紧张时想放松

   - Optimal level of arousal
      - The ideal amount of physiological activation for **best performance**, which varies by person and task.
      - 考试时适度紧张发挥最好，太放松或太焦虑都不行

   - Yerkes-Dodson Law
      - Performance increases with arousal up to a point; **easy tasks** benefit from higher arousal, **difficult tasks** benefit from lower arousal.
      - 简单任务：来点压力表现更好；困难任务：太紧张反而失常

   - Self-determination theory
      - Motivation is driven by three innate needs: **autonomy, competence, and relatedness**.
      - 人需要自主感、胜任感和归属感才能保持内在动力

   - Intrinsic motivation
      - Doing something because it is inherently <red>interesting or enjoyable</red>.
      - 因为喜欢画画而画画，不是为了获奖

   - Extrinsic motivation
      - Doing something for <red>external rewards</red> (money, grades, praise).
      - 为了奖金完成销售目标

   - Incentive theory
      - Behavior is motivated by **external stimuli** (incentives) that pull us toward them.
      - 看到美食图片就产生食欲，闻到咖啡香就想喝

   - Instincts
      - Innate, **fixed patterns** of behavior that are biologically driven (evolutionary perspective).
      - 婴儿天生会吮吸，母亲保护孩子的本能

   - Lewin's motivational conflicts theory
      - Kurt Lewin's framework describing three types of conflicts when choosing between goals.
      - 做选择时内心的纠结可以分为三种类型

   - Approach-approach conflicts
      - Choosing between **two equally attractive** options.
      - 清华还是北大，两个都想去

   - Approach-avoidance conflicts
      - A single goal has both <red>attractive and unattractive</red> aspects.
      - 想吃甜食但又怕胖

   - Avoidance-avoidance conflicts
      - Choosing between **two equally undesirable** options.
      - 补作业还是请家长，两个都不想

   - Sensation-seeking theory
      - People differ in their need for **novel, complex, and intense** stimulation.
      - 有些人天生就爱冒险、追求刺激

   - Thrill seeking
      - Seeking exciting experiences that often involve **physical risk**.
      - 跳伞、蹦极、极限运动

   - Adventure seeking
      - Desire for **novel physical activities** and exploration.
      - 喜欢旅行探险、尝试新的户外运动

   - Disinhibition
      - Seeking social stimulation through behaviors that are normally **restrained by social norms**.
      - 在派对上放飞自我，做平时不敢做的事

   - Boredom susceptibility
      - Intolerance of **repetitive, monotonous, or predictable** situations.
      - 稳定但无聊的工作干不下去，非要换个刺激的

   - Hunger
      - A motivational state driven by physiological signals (e.g., **ghrelin** from empty stomach, **hypothalamus**) and psychological/environmental cues.
      - 肚子咕咕叫是因为胃分泌了饥饿素，到了饭点也会条件反射地饿

   - Satiety
      - The feeling of **fullness** that signals the body to stop eating, regulated by hormones like **leptin**.
      - 吃饱了大脑收到瘦素信号，告诉你别吃了



4.7 - Emotion

   - Affect
      - A broad term for the experience of **feeling or emotion**.
      - 泛指所有的情绪情感体验

   - Physiological experiences
      - The **bodily responses** associated with emotion, such as increased heart rate, sweating, or muscle tension.
      - 紧张时心跳加速、手心出汗

   - Cognitive experiences
      - The **thoughts and interpretations** that accompany an emotional state.
      - 看到蛇时脑子里想到：我在危险中

   - Cognitive label
      - The <red>interpretation</red> we assign to physiological arousal, which determines the emotion we feel. Key to **Schachter-Singer Two-Factor Theory**.
      - 同样是心跳加速，在考场上解读为焦虑，在过山车上解读为兴奋

   - Facial-feedback hypothesis
      - Facial muscle movements can <red>trigger or intensify</red> the corresponding emotion.
      - 即使心情一般，强迫自己微笑也会让你感觉好一点

   - Broaden-and-build theory of emotion
      - **Positive emotions** broaden our awareness and encourage building new skills, resources, and social connections.
      - 快乐时更愿意探索新事物、交新朋友，积累更多资源

   - Cognitive appraisal
      - The process of **evaluating an event's significance** to determine the emotional response. Central to **Lazarus's theory**.
      - 你把考试定义为威胁还是挑战，决定了你后续是焦虑还是兴奋

   - Display rules
      - Cultural norms dictating **when, where, and how** emotions should be expressed.
      - 有些文化提倡喜形于色，有些文化觉得公开哭泣是不成熟的

   - Emotional elicitors
      - Stimuli or events that <red>trigger</red> emotional responses, influenced by gender, age, and culture.
      - 蛇是恐惧的诱因，收到礼物是快乐的诱因
