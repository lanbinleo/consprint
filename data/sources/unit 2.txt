2.1 - Perception

   - Bottom-up processing
      - Processing that begins with raw sensory data and builds up to perception — <red>no prior knowledge</red> involved.
      - 老外第一次看汉字，一笔一画地拆解，因为没有任何已有知识可以依赖

   - Top-down processing
      - Using <red>prior knowledge, expectations, and context</red> to interpret sensory information.
      - 研表究明汉字的序顺并不定一影阅响读，你的大脑自动用已有知识补全了意思

   - Schema
      - A mental framework that organizes knowledge and guides expectations about the world.
      - 你对餐厅的schema：进门、点餐、吃饭、买单，去到任何餐厅都会这样预期

   - Perceptual set
      - A mental predisposition to perceive things in a certain way, influenced by experience, context, culture, emotions, and motivation. <red>Not inherently positive or negative</red> — differs from mental set.
      - 先入为主，你刚看完恐怖片，走在黑暗的路上，树影都觉得像鬼

   - Gestalt psychology
      - A school of psychology emphasizing that **the whole is greater than the sum of its parts** — the brain organizes sensory information into meaningful wholes.
      - 我们看到的不是一堆孤立的点和线，而是一个完整的图形

   - Closure
      - The tendency to mentally **fill in gaps** in incomplete figures to perceive them as complete.
      - 一个缺了一块的圆，你的大脑会自动脑补成完整的圆

   - Figure and ground
      - The brain automatically separates visual input into a main object (**figure**) and its background (**ground**).
      - 经典的花瓶与人脸图，你要么看到花瓶，要么看到两张对视的脸

   - Proximity
      - Objects that are <red>close together</red> in space tend to be perceived as belonging to the same group.
      - 三个点靠在一起、另外三个点靠在一起，你会自动把它们看成两组

   - Similarity
      - Objects that <red>look alike</red> (color, shape, size) tend to be grouped together.
      - 一排红点和一排蓝点交替排列，你会按颜色把它们分成不同的组

   - Attention
      - The process of focusing conscious awareness on a particular stimulus while filtering out others.
      - 你在教室里专心听老师讲课，忽略了窗外的鸟叫声

   - Selective attention
      - Focusing on one specific stimulus while ignoring others — acts as a **filter**.
      - 你在嘈杂的自习室里专心做题，周围的聊天声你完全没在意

   - Cocktail party effect
      - The ability to detect personally relevant information (like your name) even when not actively attending to it — suggests background monitoring.
      - 嘈杂的派对上你没在听别人聊天，但远处有人叫你名字，你立刻注意到了

   - Inattentional blindness
      - Failing to notice a fully visible but unexpected stimulus when attention is focused elsewhere.
      - 经典大猩猩实验：你专注数传球次数，完全没看到一只大猩猩走过画面

   - Change blindness
      - Failing to detect obvious changes in a scene when the change occurs during a brief disruption.
      - 问路实验：你正在给一个人指路，中途被换成另一个人，你竟然没发现

   - Binocular depth cues
      - Depth cues that require <red>both eyes</red> working together, mainly for judging nearby distances.
      - 需要两只眼睛才能获得的深度信息

   - Retinal disparity
      - The slight difference in images projected on the left and right retinas; <red>greater disparity = closer object</red>.
      - 用左眼和右眼分别看手指，位置不一样，手指越近差异越大

   - Convergence
      - The inward turning of eyes when viewing a nearby object; <red>more muscle tension = closer object</red>.
      - 看很近的东西时眼睛会变成斗鸡眼，大脑通过眼部肌肉紧张程度判断远近

   - Monocular depth cues
      - Depth cues available to <red>each eye alone</red>, useful for perceiving distance in 2D images.
      - 用一只眼睛看照片也能判断远近的线索

   - Relative clarity
      - Clearer objects appear <red>closer</red>; hazier objects appear <red>farther</red> away.
      - 远处的山看起来雾蒙蒙的，近处的树看起来很清晰

   - Relative size
      - When two objects are assumed to be the same actual size, the <red>smaller image</red> appears farther away.
      - 两辆一样的车，看起来小的那辆离你更远

   - Texture gradient
      - Nearby surfaces have <red>detailed, coarse</red> textures; distant surfaces appear <red>dense and smooth</red>.
      - 脚下的草地纹理清晰可见，远处的草地纹理变得密集模糊

   - Linear perspective
      - Parallel lines appear to <red>converge</red> in the distance; more convergence = farther.
      - 铁轨在远方汇聚成一个点

   - Interposition
      - When one object <red>overlaps/blocks</red> another, the blocked object is perceived as farther away.
      - 一座楼挡住了另一座楼，被挡住的那座看起来更远

   - Apparent movement
      - The perception of movement from a rapid series of <red>still images</red> — the basis of film and animation.
      - 电影本质就是一系列静止图片快速播放，让你觉得画面在动

2.2 - Thinking, Problem-Solving, Judgments, and Decision Making

   - Prototypes
      - The <red>most typical</red> example of a concept, used as a mental reference for categorization.
      - 提到鸟，你脑中浮现的是麻雀而不是企鹅，因为麻雀更符合鸟的典型特征

   - Assimilation
      - Fitting new information <red>into an existing schema</red> without changing the schema.
      - 小孩知道狗是四条腿会叫的动物，看到新品种的狗也直接归类为狗

   - Accommodation
      - <red>Modifying an existing schema</red> to incorporate new information that doesn't fit.
      - 小孩把猫也叫狗，后来发现猫会喵喵叫，于是调整schema区分猫和狗

   - Algorithms
      - A step-by-step procedure that <red>guarantees</red> a correct solution, but may be slow.
      - 超市找薯片，一排一排货架挨个找，肯定能找到但很慢

   - Heuristics
      - Mental shortcuts that allow quick judgments; efficient but <red>not guaranteed</red> to be correct.
      - 超市找薯片，直接去零食区找，快但可能走错区域

   - Representativeness heuristic
      - Judging the likelihood of something based on how well it <red>matches a prototype</red>, often ignoring base rates.
      - 一个安静爱读书的人，你觉得他是图书管理员而不是农民，但其实农民人数远多于图书管理员

   - Availability heuristic
      - Judging frequency or probability based on how <red>easily examples come to mind</red>.
      - 新闻总报飞机失事，所以你觉得坐飞机比坐车危险，但其实车祸死亡率更高

   - Mental set
      - The tendency to use <red>previously successful strategies</red> even when they are no longer effective — leads to rigid thinking.
      - 你一直用某个数学公式解题，遇到新类型的题还是下意识套用，结果错了

   - Priming
      - Exposure to a prior stimulus <red>unconsciously influences</red> the response to a subsequent stimulus.
      - 先看到香蕉这个词，然后填 Y_L___，你更可能写出 YELLOW

   - Framing
      - The way information is <red>presented</red> significantly influences decisions and judgments.
      - 手术存活率90%听起来很安全，死亡率10%听起来很危险，其实是同一件事

   - Gambler's fallacy
      - The false belief that past random events affect future probabilities of <red>independent</red> events.
      - 连续抛了9次正面，你觉得下一次一定是反面，但每次概率都是50%

   - Sunk-cost fallacy
      - Continuing an endeavor because of <red>previously invested resources</red>, even when it's no longer rational.
      - 电影很难看，但已经花了钱买票，就强迫自己看完

   - Executive functions
      - Higher-order cognitive processes (in the **frontal lobes**) that manage attention, planning, decision-making, and self-regulation.
      - 你在写作业时控制自己不去刷手机，这就是执行功能在工作

   - Creativity
      - The ability to produce ideas that are both **novel and valuable**.
      - 用一块砖头想出几十种用途：盖房子、当哑铃、当画板……

   - Divergent thinking
      - Thinking that <red>explores multiple possible solutions</red> — open-ended and creative.
      - 头脑风暴：一块砖头有多少种用途？天马行空地想

   - Convergent thinking
      - Thinking that <red>narrows down to one correct answer</red> — logical and focused.
      - AP选择题四选一，你需要排除错误选项，找出唯一正确答案

   - Functional fixedness
      - The inability to see an object as having a function <red>beyond its typical use</red> — a type of mental set.
      - 蜡烛问题：你想不到把图钉盒倒空当烛台，因为你被它装图钉的功能固着了

2.3 - Introduction to Memory

   - Explicit memory
      - Memories that can be <red>consciously recalled</red> and verbally declared. Also called **declarative memory**.
      - 你能清楚说出昨天晚餐吃了什么，中国的首都是北京

   - Episodic memory
      - A type of explicit memory for <red>personal experiences</red> tied to specific times and places — like life episodes.
      - 你记得去年生日派对的场景、声音和当时的感受

   - Semantic memory
      - A type of explicit memory for <red>general facts and knowledge</red>, independent of personal experience.
      - 地球是圆的，1+1=2，这些知识不依赖于你的个人经历

   - Implicit memory
      - Memories that influence behavior <red>without conscious awareness</red>.
      - 你骑自行车时不需要有意识地想每一步该怎么做

   - Procedural memory
      - A type of implicit memory for <red>motor skills and habits</red> — "how-to" knowledge.
      - 骑车、打字、弹钢琴，你会做但说不清楚具体怎么协调的

   - Prospective memory
      - Remembering to <red>perform a planned action in the future</red> — "remembering to remember."
      - 记住下午三点要去取快递

   - Long-term potentiation
      - The <red>strengthening of synaptic connections</red> through repeated stimulation — the biological basis of learning and memory.
      - 这世上本没有路，走的人多了也便成了路；反复复习就是在强化神经通路

   - Working memory model
      - An updated model of STM emphasizing <red>active processing</red>, consisting of the central executive, phonological loop, and visuospatial sketchpad.
      - 短期记忆的升级版，不只是被动存放信息，而是主动加工

   - Primary memory system
      - An early term for short-term/working memory — the system that <red>actively holds</red> information currently in use.
      - 你正在使用的临时工作台，比如心算时暂时记住中间结果

   - Central executive
      - The component of working memory that <red>directs attention</red> and coordinates the phonological loop and visuospatial sketchpad.
      - 老板角色，分配注意力，协调其他两个子系统的工作

   - Phonological loop
      - The component of working memory that processes <red>auditory and verbal</red> information — the "inner ear and voice."
      - 你在脑海里默念一个电话号码来记住它

   - Visuospatial sketchpad
      - The component of working memory that processes <red>visual and spatial</red> information — the "inner eye."
      - 你在脑中想象一张地图或者旋转一个三维物体

   - Long-term memory
      - The relatively <red>permanent and unlimited</red> storage system for knowledge, skills, and experiences.
      - 你多年前学会的母语、骑车技能、童年回忆都存在这里

   - Multi-store model
      - Atkinson-Shiffrin model proposing three sequential stages: <red>sensory memory → short-term memory → long-term memory</red>.
      - 信息先进入感觉记忆，被注意后进入短期记忆，经过复述后存入长期记忆

   - Sensory memory
      - A brief storage (<red>≤1 second</red>) that holds large amounts of raw sensory input before it fades or is attended to.
      - 你快速晃动烟花棒时看到的完整光轨，就是视觉感觉记忆

   - Iconic memory
      - <red>Visual</red> sensory memory, lasting less than a second.
      - 晃动烟花棒看到完整光轨，闭眼后画面短暂停留

   - Echoic memory
      - <red>Auditory</red> sensory memory, lasting about 3-4 seconds.
      - 老师说了一句话你没听清，但几秒内可以在脑中回放刚才的声音

   - Automatic processing
      - Encoding of information <red>without conscious effort</red> — such as space, time, and frequency.
      - 你不用刻意去记，就知道今天在路上碰到了好几次同一个朋友

   - Effortful processing
      - Encoding that requires <red>conscious attention and deliberate effort</red>.
      - 背单词、复习公式，需要你集中注意力反复练习

   - Storage
      - The process of <red>maintaining</red> encoded information in memory over time.
      - 把文件保存到硬盘上

   - Levels of processing model
      - Craik & Lockhart's theory that memory depends on the <red>depth of processing</red> — deeper processing leads to better memory.
      - 加工越深记得越牢，理解意义比死记字形效果好得多

   - Shallow encoding
      - Processing information at a <red>surface level</red> (e.g., appearance or sound), producing weak memory.
      - 只注意到 Peter Parker 是大写的，或者有几个字母

   - Deep encoding
      - Processing information at a <red>meaningful level</red> (semantics), producing strong and lasting memory.
      - 看到 Peter Parker 想到他是蜘蛛侠，和已有知识建立联系

   - Structural, phonemic, semantic
      - Three levels: **structural** = physical features (shallowest), **phonemic** = sound, **semantic** = meaning (<red>deepest, most effective</red>).
      - Peter Parker：结构层看字母形状，语音层注意到两个P押头韵，语义层想到他是蜘蛛侠

2.4 - Encoding Memories

   - Encoding
      - The process of <red>converting</red> sensory information into a form that can be stored in memory.
      - 用键盘打字，把外界信息变成电子信号存进电脑

   - Mnemonic devices
      - Memory aids that use <red>strategies</red> (imagery, organization, acronyms) to enhance encoding and recall.
      - 用 My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Noodles 记太阳系行星顺序

   - Method of loci
      - A mnemonic technique that links items to <red>specific locations</red> in a familiar physical space — "memory palace."
      - 把要记的东西放在你家里不同位置，回忆时在脑中走一遍就能看到它们

   - Chunking
      - Grouping individual pieces of information into <red>larger, meaningful units</red> to overcome STM capacity limits (7±2).
      - 手机号 13812345678 分成 138-1234-5678 三组来记

   - Categories (as they pertain to grouping)
      - Organizing information into <red>meaningful groups</red> based on shared characteristics to aid encoding.
      - 购物清单分成乳制品、蔬菜、零食等类别来记

   - Hierarchies (as they pertain to grouping)
      - Organizing information in a <red>structured, ranked order</red> from broad to specific to enhance memory.
      - 生物分类：动物→哺乳动物→犬科→狗，层层细分更好记

   - Spacing effect
      - <red>Distributed practice</red> over time produces better long-term retention than massed practice.
      - 每天复习30分钟，连续一周，比考前通宵抱佛脚效果好得多

   - Memory consolidation
      - The process of converting fragile short-term memories into <red>stable long-term memories</red>, involving the **hippocampus**.
      - 电脑把RAM里的临时数据写入硬盘永久保存，睡眠对巩固记忆很重要

   - Massed practice
      - Cramming — studying intensively in <red>one concentrated session</red>; poor for long-term retention.
      - 考试前一晚通宵复习，短期可能有用但很快就忘

   - Distributed practice
      - Spreading study sessions <red>over time</red>; leads to stronger and more durable memory.
      - 每天学一点，连续一周复习，记忆效果远好于临时抱佛脚

   - Serial position effect
      - The tendency to best recall items at the <red>beginning and end</red> of a list, with poorest recall in the middle.
      - 背一长串单词，最容易记住开头和结尾的，中间的最容易忘

   - Primacy effect
      - Better recall for items at the <red>beginning</red> of a list — due to more rehearsal time, transferring them to LTM.
      - 列表开头的词你有更多时间反复默念，所以记得牢

   - Recency effect
      - Better recall for items at the <red>end</red> of a list — because they are still in working memory.
      - 列表最后几个词刚刚才听到，还留在短期记忆里所以容易想起

2.5 - Storing Memories

   - Sensory memory
      - The initial, brief storage of sensory information; <red>large capacity but very short duration</red> (≤1s for iconic, ~3-4s for echoic).
      - 记忆的接待大厅，所有感官信息先到这里，绝大部分迅速消失

   - Short-term memory
      - Temporary storage with <red>limited capacity (7±2 items)</red> and <red>limited duration (~20-30 seconds)</red> without rehearsal.
      - 临时记一个验证码，不复述的话30秒内就忘了

   - Working memory
      - The active processing system (upgraded STM) that <red>manipulates and processes</red> information, not just holds it.
      - 你做心算时，一边记住中间结果一边计算下一步

   - Long-term memory
      - The relatively <red>permanent</red> storage with virtually <red>unlimited capacity and duration</red>.
      - 你的知识、技能和所有经历的永久储存库

   - Maintenance rehearsal
      - <red>Simple repetition</red> to keep information in STM; does not effectively transfer to LTM.
      - 不停念叨验证码 583926，但挂了电话马上就忘

   - Elaborative rehearsal
      - Connecting new information to <red>existing knowledge</red> through meaningful, deep processing — effective for LTM transfer.
      - 购物清单按乳制品、清洁用品分类来记，把新信息和已有知识深度连接

   - Memory retention
      - The ability to <red>maintain</red> stored information over time.
      - 学过的知识过了一个月还能记得多少

   - Retrograde amnesia
      - Inability to recall memories from <red>before</red> a traumatic event. **Retro = backward**.
      - 车祸后醒来，不记得自己是谁，不认识家人，丢失的是过去的记忆

   - Autobiographical memory
      - Personal memory about one's own life, combining both <red>episodic and semantic</red> memories. Key: **better memory about yourself**.
      - 关于你自己人生故事的记忆，包括个人经历和关于自己的事实

   - Anterograde amnesia
      - Inability to form <red>new</red> long-term memories after a traumatic event. **Antero = forward**.
      - Henry Molaison切除海马体后，旧记忆都在，但你离开房间再回来他就不记得见过你

   - Alzheimer's disease
      - A progressive <red>neurodegenerative</red> disease characterized by memory loss, cognitive decline, and behavioral changes.
      - 从忘记最近的事开始，逐渐不认识家人、忘记个人信息、丧失自理能力

   - Infantile amnesia
      - The inability to recall personal experiences from <red>before age 3-4</red>, due to immature hippocampus, incomplete language, and undeveloped sense of self.
      - 你几乎想不起3岁前发生过什么事

2.6 - Retrieving Memories

   - Retrieval
      - The process of <red>accessing and bringing stored information</red> back into conscious awareness.
      - 从记忆库中找到并调出你需要的信息

   - Recall
      - Retrieving information <red>without</red> external cues — more difficult.
      - 填空题和简答题，没有提示，你得自己从记忆中把答案找出来

   - Recognition
      - Identifying previously learned information when it is <red>presented again</red> — easier than recall.
      - 选择题，正确答案就在眼前，你只需要认出它

   - Retrieval cues
      - Stimuli that help <red>trigger</red> the recall of stored memories; can be external (scene, smell) or internal (mood, thought).
      - 闻到某种香水的味道，突然想起一个老朋友

   - Context-dependent memory
      - Memory retrieval is better when the <red>physical environment</red> at encoding and retrieval match.
      - 在同一间教室学习和考试，你可能更容易想起复习过的内容

   - Mood-congruent memory
      - Current mood influences <red>which memories</red> are recalled — we recall memories consistent with our present mood.
      - 心情好的时候容易想起开心的事，悲伤的时候满脑子都是难过的回忆

   - State-dependent memory
      - Memory retrieval is better when the <red>physiological/psychological state</red> at encoding and retrieval match.
      - 学习时喝了咖啡处于兴奋状态，考试前也喝一杯可能帮你更好地回忆

   - Testing effect
      - <red>Self-testing</red> (retrieval practice) strengthens long-term memory more effectively than simply re-reading material.
      - 做练习题、考自己、向别人解释概念，比反复看书效果好得多

   - Metacognition
      - <red>Thinking about your own thinking</red> — monitoring and reflecting on your cognitive processes.
      - 你意识到自己没听懂老师讲的内容，决定换一种方式学习

2.7 - Forgetting and Other Memory Challenges

   - Forgetting curve
      - Ebbinghaus's finding that forgetting occurs rapidly after learning (<red>~70% lost within 24 hours</red>) and then levels off. Countered by **distributed practice**.
      - 新知识学完24小时内忘掉大部分，不复习的话考完试就还给老师了

   - Encoding failure
      - Information was <red>never properly encoded</red> into LTM in the first place — not truly forgetting, but never stored.
      - 你天天用硬币但说不出硬币上的头像朝哪边，因为从来没认真编码过

   - Proactive interference
      - <red>Old</red> memories interfere with the retrieval of <red>new</red> information. **Pro = forward** (old → new).
      - 搬家了填表格时下意识写出旧地址，旧记忆干扰了新记忆

   - Retroactive interference
      - <red>New</red> memories interfere with the retrieval of <red>old</red> information. **Retro = backward** (new → old).
      - 学了一首新曲子后，发现想不起以前那首旧曲子的旋律了

   - Tip-of-the-tongue phenomenon
      - The feeling of <red>knowing</red> information but being temporarily unable to retrieve it — available but not retrievable.
      - 那个人叫什么来着……就在嘴边就是说不出来，过一会突然想起来了

   - Repression (psychodynamic)
      - The <red>unconscious</red> pushing of painful or traumatic memories out of awareness as a defense mechanism. **Controversial** — lacks strong scientific support.
      - 弗洛伊德认为人会无意识地将创伤记忆排除出意识来保护自己，但这个概念在现代心理学中有争议

   - Misinformation effect
      - Exposure to <red>misleading post-event information</red> distorts the original memory.
      - 车祸实验：用 smashed 代替 hit 来提问，参与者估计的车速更高，还声称看到了不存在的碎玻璃

   - Source amnesia
      - Remembering information but <red>forgetting where, when, or how</red> it was learned.
      - 你记得一个冷知识但想不起是从哪知道的，既视感（déjà vu）可能也与此有关

   - Constructive memory
      - Memory is not simply retrieved but <red>actively reconstructed</red> each time, filling gaps with existing knowledge, beliefs, and expectations.
      - 每次回忆都是一次重新建构，你会用已有经验自动填补记忆中的空白

   - Imagination inflation
      - <red>Imagining</red> a fictitious event increases confidence that it actually happened.
      - 反复想象自己小时候在商场走丢，即使从未发生，你可能会觉得这事真的发生过

2.8 - Intelligence and Achievement

   - Intelligence
      - The ability to <red>learn from experience, solve problems, and adapt</red> to new situations; considered adaptive, involving multiple mental processes, and culture-specific.
      - 没有统一定义，但一般认为是学习能力、解决问题和适应新环境的能力

   - g (general intelligence)
      - Spearman's concept of a single <red>underlying general intelligence factor</red> that influences performance across all cognitive tasks.
      - 好学生啥都好，一项认知测试强的人其他方面往往也不错

   - Intelligence quotient (IQ)
      - A standardized score representing intellectual ability, with <red>100 as the average</red>, following a normal distribution.
      - 智商分数，100分为平均值，用来和同龄人比较

   - Mental age
      - The age level of intellectual performance a person demonstrates on a test, <red>regardless of actual age</red>.
      - 一个8岁孩子的测试表现和平均10岁孩子一样好，他的心理年龄就是10岁

   - Chronological age
      - A person's <red>actual age</red> in years from birth.
      - 你的实际年龄，用来和心理年龄对比计算IQ

   - Standardization
      - Establishing <red>uniform procedures</red> for administering and scoring a test, with norms from a representative sample.
      - 所有考生在相同条件下考试，用统一的评分标准改卷，分数才有可比性

   - Validity
      - The extent to which a test <red>measures what it claims to measure</red>.
      - 不能站在体重秤上测身高，体重秤测身高就是无效的

   - Construct validity
      - Whether a test accurately measures the <red>theoretical psychological concept</red> it intends to measure (e.g., anxiety, intelligence).
      - 一个智力测试是否真的在测量智力这个心理学概念

   - Predictive validity
      - Whether test scores can effectively <red>predict future behavior</red> or performance.
      - SAT分数能否预测大学GPA

   - Reliability
      - The extent to which a test yields <red>consistent, reproducible</red> results.
      - 能力没变化的情况下，测两次应该得到相近的分数

   - Test-retest reliability
      - Consistency of scores when the <red>same test is given twice</red> to the same group at different times.
      - 同一组人隔一个月测两次，看同一个人的分数是否接近

   - Split-half reliability
      - Consistency between <red>two halves</red> of a single test (e.g., odd vs. even items).
      - 把试卷的奇数题和偶数题分数做比较，高相关说明在测量同样的东西

   - Stereotype threat
      - Anxiety that one's performance will <red>confirm a negative stereotype</red> about their group, which impairs performance.
      - 女生数学不好的刻板印象让女生在数学考试时更紧张，反而考得更差

   - Stereotype lift
      - <red>Improved</red> performance resulting from awareness of a positive stereotype about one's group.
      - 亚洲人数学好的刻板印象让亚洲学生在数学考试时更自信

   - Flynn Effect
      - The observed trend that average IQ scores have been <red>rising across generations</red> worldwide — requiring periodic restandardization.
      - 全球IQ平均分一代比一代高，可能因为营养、教育和科技进步

   - Achievement tests
      - Tests that measure <red>what a person has already learned</red>.
      - AP考试、期末考试、单元测验

   - Aptitude tests
      - Tests designed to <red>predict future learning ability</red> or potential, not current knowledge.
      - SAT预测你未来在大学的学习潜力

   - Fixed mindset
      - The belief that intelligence is <red>innate and unchangeable</red>; failure = lack of ability.
      - 觉得智力天生固定，害怕挑战，因为失败意味着自己不聪明

   - Growth mindset
      - The belief that intelligence can be <red>developed through effort and learning</red>; failure = opportunity to grow.
      - 觉得能力可以通过努力提升，失败是学习和成长的机会
