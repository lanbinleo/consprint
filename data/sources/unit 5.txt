5.1 - Introduction to Health Psychology

 - Health psychology
    - A subfield of psychology focused on promoting health and preventing/treating illness, including **psychological responses** to being sick.
    - 研究人生病时的心理反应、应对方式，以及如何改善医疗系统
 - Stress
    - A state of **physiological and psychological tension** caused by stressors. Chronic stress weakens the immune system.
    - 期末考试周感到身心俱疲、睡不好觉
 - Hypertension
    - **High blood pressure**, a common physical consequence of chronic stress.
    - 长期高压工作导致血压升高
 - Immune suppression
    - Chronic stress **weakens the immune system**, making the body more vulnerable to illness.
    - 考试周压力太大反而感冒了
 - Stressors
    - Events or conditions in the environment that **trigger** the stress response.
    - 考试、失恋、经济困难、噪音
 - Eustress (motivating)
    - **Positive stress** from beneficial events that provides motivation and enhances performance.
    - 比赛前的兴奋紧张感让你发挥更好
 - Distress (debilitating)
    - **Negative stress** that impairs motivation, performance, and causes emotional/physical harm.
    - 亲人去世、失业带来的持续痛苦
 - Adverse childhood experiences (ACEs)
    - Potentially **traumatic events occurring before age 18** that can have lasting negative effects.
    - 童年时期经历家暴、父母酗酒、被忽视
 - General adaptation syndrome (GAS)
    - <red>Hans Selye</red> 提出的三阶段压力反应模型：**Alarm → Resistance → Exhaustion**，描述身体在持续压力下如何逐步耗竭。
    - 刚接到坏消息时心跳加速（警觉）→ 几周后似乎适应了（抵抗）→ 几个月后身体垮了（衰竭）
 - Alarm reaction phase
    - The **initial reaction** to a stressor; the body activates the fight-or-flight response, releasing adrenaline and cortisol.
    - 突然被老师点名，心跳猛地加速、手心出汗
 - Resistance phase
    - The body attempts to **adapt and cope** with the ongoing stressor; appears to be managing but resources are being consumed.
    - 连续加班几周，表面上还撑得住，但身体已经在消耗储备
 - Fight-flight-freeze response
    - Activation of the **sympathetic nervous system**: heart rate increases, adrenaline surges, preparing the body to fight, flee, or freeze.
    - 走夜路遇到可疑的人，瞬间心跳加速、腿发软跑不动（freeze）
 - Exhaustion phase
    - Body's resources are **depleted** after prolonged stress, leading to weakened immunity, burnout, and vulnerability to illness.
    - 长期高压工作后免疫力崩溃，反复生病、极度疲劳
 - Tend-and-befriend theory
    - A stress response (especially in women) involving **nurturing others (tend)** and **seeking social support (befriend)**, rather than fighting or fleeing.
    - 地震后母亲先安抚孩子，然后联系亲友互相支持
 - Problem-focused coping
    - Coping by **directly addressing the stressor** — changing the situation or one's interaction with it.
    - 考试压力大 → 制定详细的复习计划来解决问题
 - Emotion-focused coping
    - Coping by **managing emotional reactions** when the stressor itself cannot be changed.
    - 亲人去世无法改变 → 通过冥想、深呼吸、找朋友倾诉来缓解悲伤


5.2 - Positive Psychology

 - Positive psychology
    - The scientific study of **optimal human functioning**, advocated by <red>Martin Seligman</red>. Focuses on strengths, virtues, and what helps people thrive.
    - 不只研究心理疾病，而是研究什么让人活得好、活得有意义
 - Well-being
    - A broad concept encompassing **physical, emotional, and social health** — an overall sense of flourishing.
    - 身体健康、情绪稳定、人际关系良好的综合状态
 - Resilience
    - The ability to **recover and bounce back** from adversity, trauma, or stress.
    - 创业失败后重新振作，从头再来
 - Positive emotions
    - Emotions such as joy, gratitude, and hope that **broaden thinking** and build psychological resources.
    - 帮助别人后感到的满足和快乐
 - Gratitude
    - A positive emotion emphasized in PP; research shows regularly expressing gratitude **significantly improves long-term well-being**.
    - 每天写三件感恩的事，长期坚持后幸福感明显提升
 - Positive subjective experiences
    - **Internal, self-reported** experiences of happiness, satisfaction, and fulfillment.
    - 回忆一次旅行时内心涌起的幸福感
 - Subjective well-being
    - A person's **self-evaluation** of their life satisfaction and happiness — how happy *they feel* they are.
    - 收入不高但觉得自己生活很幸福，主观幸福感就高
 - Signature strengths
    - The **core character strengths** most central to a person's identity, organized under six virtues. Using them boosts well-being.
    - 一个人最突出的特质可能是好奇心和创造力
 - Virtues
    - Six broad categories under which signature strengths are organized: **Wisdom, Courage, Humanity, Justice, Temperance, Transcendence**.
    - 智慧（好奇心）、勇气（毅力）、人道（善良）、正义（公平）、节制（自控）、超越（感恩、希望）
 - Positive objective experiences
    - **External, measurable** indicators of well-being, such as income, education, health status.
    - 稳定的收入、良好的健康指标、受教育程度
 - Happiness
    - A state of well-being characterized by positive emotions and life satisfaction; influenced by both **genetics and circumstances**.
    - 做自己喜欢的事时感到的愉悦和满足
 - Categories of virtues
    - The six categories: **Wisdom, Courage, Humanity, Justice, Temperance, Transcendence** — each containing specific character strengths.
    - 同上 Virtues
 - Posttraumatic growth
    - **Positive psychological change** that emerges after struggling with a major crisis or trauma — deeper understanding of life, stronger relationships, increased personal strength.
    - 经历重病后更珍惜生活，人际关系变得更深刻，内心更强大


5.3 - Explaining and Classifying Psychological Disorders

 - Dysfunction
    - One of the <red>Four Ds</red>: inability to **maintain daily functioning** — self-care, social relationships, or work.
    - 抑郁到无法起床上学、无法维持基本社交
 - Distress
    - One of the Four Ds: the individual experiences **subjective suffering** from their own thoughts, emotions, or behaviors.
    - 强迫症患者因反复洗手感到极度痛苦
 - Deviation from the social norm
    - One of the Four Ds (**Deviance**): behavior that significantly departs from cultural/social norms, with **statistical infrequency**. 但罕见≠障碍（天才也罕见）。
    - 在公共场合对着空气大声说话
 - Cultural/societal norms
    - Standards of acceptable behavior that **vary across cultures and time periods**; what is "abnormal" is partly defined by these norms.
    - 同性恋曾被DSM列为障碍，1980年被移除——说明诊断标准随社会价值观变化
 - Stigma
    - **Negative social attitudes** toward mental illness, leading to stereotypes, prejudice, and discrimination.
    - 别人知道你看心理医生后对你另眼相看、疏远你
 - Racism
    - Prejudice and discrimination based on **race/ethnicity**.
    - 因种族背景在求职中被歧视
 - Sexism
    - Prejudice and discrimination based on **sex/gender**.
    - 女性在职场中因性别被低估能力
 - Ageism
    - Prejudice and discrimination based on **age**.
    - 认为老年人学不会新技术
 - Discrimination
    - **Behavioral** component of prejudice — unfair treatment of individuals based on group membership.
    - 因心理疾病诊断标签而被拒绝录用
 - Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM)
    - Published by the **APA** (American Psychiatric Association). Current version is <red>DSM-5</red> (2013), containing **541 diagnoses**. Serves as the standard classification system in the US and basis for insurance.
    - 1952年首版仅128种诊断；1980年删除同性恋条目
 - International Classification of Mental Disorders (ICD)
    - Published by the **WHO**; a globally used classification system for clinical descriptions and diagnostic codes.
    - 全球通用的疾病分类标准，不限于心理障碍
 - Eclectic approach
    - Rejecting single-factor explanations; **integrating multiple perspectives** (biological, psychological, social) to understand and treat disorders.
    - 治疗抑郁症时同时考虑药物、认知疗法和社会支持
 - Behavioral perspective
    - Psychological disorders result from **maladaptive learned associations** through classical and operant conditioning.
    - 被狗咬过后对所有狗产生恐惧（经典条件反射）
 - Maladaptive learned associations
    - Unhealthy **stimulus-response connections** formed through learning that maintain problematic behavior.
    - 每次焦虑就喝酒缓解，形成了不健康的应对联结
 - Humanistic perspective
    - Disorders arise when **self-actualization is blocked** or growth environment is deficient; emphasizes unconditional positive regard.
    - 一个人因长期被否定而无法发挥潜能，需要无条件的接纳和支持
 - Cognitive perspective
    - Disorders stem from **maladaptive thinking patterns** and cognitive distortions; treatment involves identifying and correcting negative automatic thoughts.
    - 一次考试没考好就认为自己什么都做不好（以偏概全的认知扭曲）
 - Psychodynamic perspective
    - Disorders originate from **unconscious conflicts and childhood experiences**; treatment involves uncovering and processing these.
    - 童年被忽视的经历导致成年后的信任问题
 - Evolutionary perspective
    - Certain psychological traits or disorders may have been **adaptive in ancestral environments**.
    - 焦虑反应在远古时期帮助人类警觉危险、存活下来
 - Sociocultural perspective
    - Disorders arise from conflicts between the individual and **social/cultural norms and environments**.
    - 移民因文化冲突产生适应障碍
 - Biological perspective
    - Disorders result from **neurochemical imbalances, genetic factors, or brain structure abnormalities**; treated with medication, stimulation, or surgery.
    - 抑郁症与血清素水平低有关，用药物调节神经递质
 - Biopsychosocial model
    - An **integrative model**: disorders result from the interaction of <red>biological × psychological × social</red> factors. The mainstream approach in modern clinical practice.
    - 抑郁症 = 基因易感性（bio）+ 消极思维模式（psych）+ 社会孤立（social）
 - Diathesis-stress model
    - Disorders are triggered when **innate genetic vulnerability (diathesis/nature)** interacts with **environmental stressors (stress/nurture)**. Having a family history ≠ inevitably developing the disorder.
    - 有精神分裂症家族史（素质），但只有在经历重大压力事件后才发病（压力触发）

5.4 - Selection of Categories of Psychological Disorders


 - Neurodevelopmental disorders
    - A group of brain dysfunctions present at **birth or early childhood**, affecting behavior, memory, attention, and/or learning. Causes include genetic, physiological, and environmental factors.
    - ADHD和ASD都属于这一类
 - Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD)
    - Characterized by persistent patterns of **inattention** and/or **hyperactivity-impulsivity** that interfere with functioning. Requires ≥6 symptoms for ≥6 months, onset **before age 12**, across multiple settings.
    - 上课无法集中注意力，坐不住，经常打断别人说话
 - Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)
    - Characterized by deficits in **social-emotional reciprocity, nonverbal communication, and relationship maintenance**, plus **restricted/repetitive** behaviors, interests, or activities. Onset in early childhood.
    - 不理解社交暗示，对特定话题有极度强烈的兴趣，重复同样的动作


 - Schizophrenic Spectrum Disorders
    - A <red>psychotic disorder</red> involving deterioration in functioning due to abnormal perceptions, bizarre thinking, emotional dysregulation, and motor abnormalities. 不是人格分裂（split mind 是心智的分裂）。Diagnosis requires ≥2 symptoms for ≥1 month, with ≥5 months of impairment.
    - 一个人出现幻觉、妄想，无法正常工作和社交
 - Positive symptoms
    - Symptoms that represent an <red>excess or addition</red> to normal functioning — things present that shouldn't be.
    - 妄想、幻觉、言语紊乱、紧张症
 - Delusions
    - **False beliefs** maintained despite contradictory evidence.
    - 坚信自己被监视，即使没有任何证据
 - Delusions of persecution
    - Belief that one is being **persecuted, watched, or conspired against** by others. 最常见的妄想类型。
    - 坚信有人在食物里下毒
 - Delusions of grandeur
    - Belief that one is an **extraordinarily powerful or important** person with special abilities.
    - 相信自己是某个王室的继承人
 - Hallucinations
    - **False sensory perceptions** that feel vivid and real; content is usually negative. <red>Auditory hallucinations</red> are the most common type. Research shows the brain actually generates neural signals that produce the sounds.
    - 听到有声音命令自己做事，但周围没有人
 - Disorganized thinking
    - Disrupted thought processes reflected in incoherent or illogical patterns of reasoning.
    - 无法按逻辑组织想法，思维跳跃混乱
 - Disorganized speech
    - Incoherent or meaningless speech patterns, such as jumping between unrelated topics or inappropriate word use.
    - 说话完全没有逻辑，听的人无法理解
 - Word salad
    - Completely **scrambled, incoherent speech** — an extreme form of disorganized speech.
    - 苹果飞机紫色跳舞……完全无法理解的词语堆砌
 - Disorganized motor behavior
    - Excessive, **purposeless motor activity** including agitation, bizarre postures, pacing, repetitive movements, or even aggression.
    - 反复踱步、模仿别人的动作、摆出怪异姿势
 - Catatonia
    - As a positive symptom: excessive purposeless movement. As a negative symptom: **lack of movement or responsiveness** (catatonic stupor).
    - 阳性：不停踱步、做重复动作；阴性：保持一个诡异的姿势一动不动
 - Negative symptoms
    - Symptoms that represent a <red>reduction or absence</red> of normal functioning. 可能被误认为抑郁或懒惰。
    - 情感平淡、缺乏动力、社交退缩
 - Flat affect
    - Severely **diminished emotional expression**: unchanging facial expression, poor eye contact, monotone voice, reduced gestures.
    - 听到好消息或坏消息时表情完全没有变化，说话语调平平
 - Catatonic stupor
    - A negative symptom: **lack of movement or responsiveness** to the environment; may hold unusual postures and resist being moved.
    - 像雕塑一样保持一个姿势几个小时不动，不回应任何人
 - Dopamine hypothesis
    - The theory that **highly fluctuating dopamine levels** contribute to schizophrenia symptoms. Dopamine is the key neurotransmitter associated with schizophrenia.
    - 多巴胺水平异常波动导致幻觉和妄想等症状


 - Depressive disorders
    - A category of mood disorders characterized by persistent **low mood, loss of motivation**, and impaired functioning.
    - 持续的悲伤、对事物失去兴趣
 - Major Depressive Disorder
    - <red>Unipolar</red> depression: intense depressive mood, decreased interest/pleasure, energy loss, and decision-making problems for **at least 2 weeks**. Requires depressed mood or loss of interest plus ≥3-4 additional symptoms.
    - 连续两周以上每天都感到极度悲伤，无法上学上班，反复想到死亡
 - Persistent Depressive Disorder
    - A chronic, **longer-lasting** form of depression lasting **at least 2 years**, with symptoms such as appetite changes, sleep disturbances, low energy, and negative self-perception.
    - 两年多来大部分时间都情绪低落，虽然没有重度抑郁那么严重，但一直好不了


 - Bipolar disorders
    - Mood disorders involving **alternating episodes** between depression and mania.
    - 情绪在极度亢奋和极度低落之间来回波动
 - Cycling
    - The **switching between manic and depressive states**; duration and intensity can vary.
    - 这个月躁狂、下个月抑郁，反复循环
 - Mania
    - Elevated mood, **increased energy**, decreased need for sleep, racing thoughts, impulsive/risky behavior, inflated self-esteem, more talkative and sociable.
    - 连续几天不睡觉但精力充沛，疯狂消费，觉得自己无所不能
 - Depression
    - Persistent sadness, fatigue, insomnia or hypersomnia, difficulty concentrating, feelings of worthlessness, **withdrawal** from activities and people, suicidal thoughts.
    - 躁狂期过后陷入极度疲惫和绝望，不想见任何人
 - Bipolar I Disorder
    - Characterized by at least one full **manic episode**; may have hypomanic or major depressive episodes. <red>More severe</red> than Bipolar II.
    - 经历过完整的躁狂发作，可能住院治疗
 - Bipolar II Disorder
    - Characterized by at least one **hypomanic episode** and one major depressive episode; **never** a full manic episode. Less severe than Bipolar I.
    - 有过轻躁狂（情绪高涨但没有严重到影响功能）和重度抑郁发作


 - Anxiety disorders
    - The **most common** psychological disorders in the US (19 million adults). Pathological anxiety is disruptive, irrational, and uncontrollable.
    - 焦虑本身正常，但病理性焦虑是过度的、无法控制的
 - Generalized anxiety disorder (GAD)
    - Excessive anxiety about **almost everything** for ≥6 months, with ≥3 of: edginess, fatigue, poor concentration, irritability, muscle tension, sleep problems. <red>Free-floating anxiety</red> — no specific trigger.
    - 什么都担心，工作、健康、家人，但说不清楚到底怕什么
 - Panic disorder
    - Characterized by recurrent, **unpredictable panic attacks** with severe physical symptoms (chest pain, choking, sweating). Lasting minutes to hours, no specific trigger. Requires ≥1 month of worry about recurrence or maladaptive behavioral changes.
    - 突然心跳加速、胸痛、感觉快要窒息，以为自己心脏病发作
 - Panic attack
    - An episode of **intense, acute anxiety** with severe physical symptoms such as chest pain, sweating, choking, and muscle tightness.
    - 突然感到极度恐惧，全身发抖、呼吸困难，持续几分钟
 - Ataque de nervios
    - A <red>culture-bound syndrome</red> common in **Latin American and Mediterranean** cultures, characterized by emotional outbursts — screaming, crying, trembling, or fainting — as a response to intense emotional events.
    - 在拉丁文化中，亲人去世后出现尖叫、哭泣、颤抖甚至昏厥的情绪爆发
 - Specific phobia
    - An intense, **irrational fear** of a specific stimulus, lasting ≥6 months, causing immediate fear upon exposure and avoidance behavior.
    - 看到蜘蛛就尖叫逃跑，明知过度但无法控制
 - Acrophobia
    - Fear of **heights**.
    - 站在高楼阳台上就双腿发软、头晕
 - Arachnophobia
    - Fear of **spiders**.
    - 看到蜘蛛就极度恐慌
 - Agoraphobia
    - Fear of **open or public spaces** where escape might be difficult.
    - 害怕去商场、广场等开阔公共场所
 - Social anxiety disorder
    - Intense fear and anxiety in **social situations** where one might be scrutinized; fear of being negatively evaluated. Lasts ≥6 months, disproportionate to actual threat.
    - 在课堂上被叫起来回答问题时极度恐惧，害怕被同学笑话
 - Taijin kyofusho
    - A <red>culture-bound syndrome</red> from **Japan**: intense fear of **offending or embarrassing others** with one's appearance or behavior (contrast with Western social anxiety which focuses on fear of being judged oneself).
    - 日本文化中，极度害怕自己的存在让别人感到不舒服


 - Obsessive-compulsive and related disorders
    - A category of disorders involving **obsessions, compulsions, or related repetitive behaviors** that create distress and interfere with daily functioning. Includes conditions such as OCD and hoarding disorder.
    - 一类以强迫观念、强迫行为或相关重复行为为核心的障碍，会带来痛苦并影响日常生活，包括强迫症和囤积障碍
 - Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
    - A disorder involving both **obsessions** (intrusive, unwanted thoughts) and **compulsions** (ritualistic behaviors performed to reduce anxiety). Time-consuming and causes significant distress.
    - 反复想到门没锁好（强迫观念），于是回去检查五六次（强迫行为）
 - Obsessions
    - Persistent, intrusive, **unwanted thoughts** the individual cannot get rid of: contamination fears, harm fears, need for symmetry, intrusive violent/sexual thoughts.
    - 脑海里不停出现自己会伤害家人的画面，越想停越停不下来
 - Compulsions
    - **Repetitive ritualistic behaviors** performed to reduce the anxiety caused by obsessions: excessive washing, checking, arranging, counting, hoarding.
    - 每天洗手几十次直到手都裂了
 - Hoarding disorder
    - Persistent difficulty **discarding possessions** due to a perceived need to save them, causing severe clutter and significant distress or impairment.
    - 家里堆满了旧报纸和塑料袋，完全无法丢弃，连走路的空间都没有


 - Dissociative disorders
    - Characterized by **disruptions in consciousness, memory, identity, or perception**, causing significant impairment. Dissociation itself can be normal (e.g., highway hypnosis).
    - 开车时进入自动驾驶状态，到达目的地后完全不记得路上发生了什么
 - Dissociation
    - A **disconnection** from one's thoughts, feelings, surroundings, or identity; can range from normal (daydreaming) to pathological.
    - 压力极大时感觉自己灵魂出窍，像在看别人的生活
 - Dissociative amnesia
    - Inability to recall **important autobiographical information**, usually of a traumatic nature — beyond ordinary forgetting.
    - 经历车祸后完全记不起事故发生的过程
 - Dissociative identity disorder (DID)
    - Presence of **≥2 distinct personality states**, each with its own way of perceiving and interacting with the world, causing memory gaps. Often associated with severe childhood physical/sexual abuse.
    - 一个人有多个不同的人格，彼此之间可能互不知道对方的存在


 - Trauma and stressor-related disorders
    - Disorders involving psychological distress after exposure to traumatic/stressful events, featuring hypervigilance, severe anxiety, flashbacks, insomnia, emotional detachment, and hostility.
    - 经历战争或灾难后出现一系列心理症状
 - Hypervigilance
    - A state of **heightened alertness** and scanning for threats.
    - 退伍军人听到巨响就立刻趴下寻找掩护
 - Flashbacks
    - **Intrusive, vivid re-experiencing** of traumatic events as if they are happening again.
    - 突然感觉自己又回到了事故现场，画面和声音都栩栩如生
 - Insomnia
    - Difficulty **falling or staying asleep**, commonly associated with trauma-related disorders.
    - 经历创伤后每晚都难以入睡，反复惊醒
 - Emotional detachment
    - A state of **emotional numbness** or disconnection from one's feelings and from others.
    - 经历创伤后对任何事都感觉麻木，无法感受到快乐或悲伤
 - Hostility
    - Increased **irritability and aggression** as a symptom of trauma-related disorders.
    - 创伤后变得暴躁易怒，经常对家人发脾气
 - Posttraumatic stress disorder (PTSD)
    - Persistent psychological distress after trauma exposure, characterized by <red>intrusive memories/flashbacks, avoidance of reminders, negative cognitive/emotional changes, and heightened reactivity</red>. Symptoms last **>1 month** and cause significant impairment. Onset can be delayed months or years.
    - 士兵从战场回来后反复做噩梦，回避任何与战争相关的事物，听到烟花声就恐慌


 - Feeding and eating disorders
    - Disorders defined by **altered food consumption or absorption** — too little or too much — impairing health and psychological functioning.
    - 饮食行为严重异常，影响身体健康和心理功能
 - Anorexia nervosa
    - Characterized by **restricted food intake**, intense fear of gaining weight, and **distorted body image**, leading to significant weight loss and health complications.
    - 已经很瘦了但照镜子觉得自己很胖，极度节食
 - Bulimia nervosa
    - Characterized by recurrent **binge eating** followed by inappropriate compensatory behaviors (self-induced vomiting, fasting, excessive exercise) to prevent weight gain.
    - 一次性吃掉大量食物，然后跑到厕所催吐


 - Personality disorders
    - Long-standing, **rigid, maladaptive** patterns of thinking and behavior that impair social functioning. Individuals typically lack insight. <red>Not diagnosed before age 18</red> because personality is still developing. Must deviate markedly from cultural norms.
    - 一个人长期的行为模式极端且僵化，影响人际关系，但自己意识不到问题
 - Cluster A (odd or eccentric)
    - 关键词：<red>Weird</red>（怪异）
 - Paranoid personality disorder
    - Pattern of **distrust and suspicion** of others' motives; accusatory; misreads others' intentions.
    - 总觉得朋友在背后说自己坏话，从不向任何人吐露心事
 - Schizoid personality disorder
    - Persistent **avoidance of social relationships** and very little emotional expression; genuinely prefers being alone; appears **cold/aloof**. More common in males.
    - 真心喜欢独处，对交朋友和谈恋爱毫无兴趣
 - Schizotypal personality disorder
    - **Bizarre thinking, perceptions, and eccentric behavior**; distorted thinking; avoids close relationships.
    - 相信自己有心灵感应能力，穿着打扮非常怪异，让人感觉很奇怪
 - Cluster B (dramatic, emotional, or erratic)
    - 关键词：<red>Wild</red>（狂野）
 - Antisocial personality disorder (APD)
    - Pattern of **disregarding and violating others' rights**; manipulative, exploitative, irresponsible; no guilt. Closely linked to criminal behavior. More common in males.
    - 反复欺骗和利用他人，完全没有愧疚感，有犯罪记录
 - Borderline personality disorder
    - **Instability** in relationships, self-image, and emotions, plus **impulsivity**; unpredictable; suicidal threats common. More common in females.
    - 对朋友的态度一天之内从极度依赖变成极度厌恶，经常用自伤威胁别人
 - Histrionic personality disorder
    - Pattern of **excessive emotionality** (dramatic) and **attention-seeking**; self-centered; exaggerates. More common in females.
    - 说话夸张戏剧化，总要成为全场焦点，稍微被忽视就受不了
 - Narcissistic personality disorder
    - Pattern of **grandiosity, need for admiration, and lack of empathy**; cannot accept criticism; expects special treatment. More common in males.
    - 觉得自己比所有人都优秀，要求特殊对待，完全不在乎别人的感受
 - Cluster C (anxious or fearful)
    - 关键词：<red>Worried</red>（担忧）
 - Avoidant personality disorder
    - Persistent **discomfort in social situations**, overwhelming feelings of inadequacy, and extreme sensitivity to **rejection/negative evaluation**.
    - 很想交朋友但极度害怕被拒绝，所以干脆回避一切社交
 - Dependent personality disorder
    - Pattern of **clinging, submissive behavior**; fear of separation; constant need to be cared for; lets others make all decisions. More common in females.
    - 什么事都让别人替自己决定，极度害怕被抛弃
 - Obsessive-compulsive personality disorder
    - Intense preoccupation with **orderliness, perfectionism, and control**, to the point of losing flexibility and efficiency. 注意：这是人格障碍，和OCD不同。
    - 对规则和细节极度执着，一份报告改了二十遍还不满意，反而什么都完不成


5.5 - Treatment of Psychological Disorders


 - Evidence-based interventions
    - Treatment methods supported by **scientific research and empirical evidence**, ensuring effectiveness and reliability.
    - 使用经过临床试验验证有效的疗法，而不是凭感觉治疗
 - Cultural humility
    - Ongoing **self-reflection** and learning about clients' cultural backgrounds to provide culturally sensitive treatment.
    - 治疗师主动了解来访者的文化背景，尊重文化差异
 - Therapeutic alliance
    - The **collaborative, trusting relationship** between therapist and client — a key factor in treatment effectiveness regardless of approach.
    - 来访者感到被治疗师理解和信任，愿意敞开心扉
 - Nonmaleficence
    - <red>Do no harm</red> — avoid causing physical, emotional, or psychological harm to clients.
    - 治疗师不能使用可能伤害来访者的方法
 - Fidelity
    - Maintaining **trust, honesty, and commitment** in the therapeutic relationship.
    - 信守与来访者的咨询契约和承诺
 - Integrity
    - Maintaining **honesty, transparency, and consistency** in professional conduct.
    - 对来访者坦诚，不隐瞒治疗方案的利弊
 - Respect for people's rights and dignity
    - Protecting clients' **privacy, confidentiality, and autonomy** — including the right to pause or stop treatment.
    - 来访者有权随时暂停或终止治疗
 - Psychotropic medication
    - Medications that affect **brain chemistry** to treat psychological disorders; based on the premise that many disorders involve biological factors.
    - 通过影响神经递质来缓解心理障碍的症状


 - Psychodynamic therapies
    - Based on **psychoanalysis**: uncovering unconscious content to relieve psychological tension. Traditional format: 2-3 sessions/week, up to 7 years.
    - 通过探索潜意识冲突和童年经历来理解当前的心理问题
 - Free association
    - Client freely expresses whatever comes to mind — thoughts, feelings, memories — to **uncover unconscious conflicts**. <red>Freudian slips</red> (口误) are seen as revealing true thoughts.
    - 来访者躺在沙发上想到什么就说什么，治疗师从中寻找潜意识线索
 - Dream interpretation
    - Analyzing dream content to reveal **unconscious thoughts and unresolved conflicts**.
    - 通过分析梦境中反复出现的场景来发现潜意识中的恐惧


 - Cognitive therapies
    - Based on the idea that **thinking colors our feelings** — thoughts mediate between events and emotional reactions.
    - 改变你对事件的想法，就能改变你的情绪反应
 - Cognitive restructuring
    - Identifying and replacing **negative, irrational beliefs** with more positive, realistic thoughts.
    - 把考试没考好就觉得自己一无是处的想法，替换为这只是一次考试，下次可以改进
 - Combating maladaptive thinking
    - Identifying and challenging **negative, irrational thought patterns** that cause emotional distress and dysfunction.
    - 意识到自己总是灾难化思维（一件小事就觉得天要塌了），然后主动纠正
 - Cognitive triad
    - <red>Beck's</red> concept: negative views of **self, world, and future** — the core of depression.
    - 我不行（自我）、世界很糟糕（世界）、未来没有希望（未来）
 - Fear hierarchies
    - A ranked list of feared situations from **least to most anxiety-provoking**, used in systematic desensitization.
    - 怕蜘蛛：看蜘蛛图片（低）→ 看真蜘蛛（中）→ 触摸蜘蛛（高）


 - Applied behavior analysis
    - The application of **learning principles** (classical and operant conditioning) to modify behavior.
    - 用行为学原理系统地改变问题行为
 - Exposure therapies
    - Gradual, repeated exposure to feared objects/situations in a **controlled environment** to reduce anxiety. Applicable to phobias, panic disorder, social anxiety, OCD, PTSD, GAD.
    - 让恐高症患者从低楼层开始逐步适应高度
 - Systematic desensitization
    - Combines a **fear hierarchy** with progressive **relaxation training**; based on the principle of <red>reciprocal inhibition</red> — you cannot be deeply relaxed and deeply afraid at the same time.
    - 先学会深度放松，然后在放松状态下从最轻微的恐惧场景开始逐步面对
 - Aversion therapies
    - Pairing an **unwanted behavior with an unpleasant stimulus** to create a negative association. Can be harmful if improperly administered.
    - 让酗酒者在喝酒时同时服用引起恶心的药物，建立喝酒=恶心的联结
 - Token economies
    - Using **tokens as rewards** for desired behaviors (self-care, medication compliance); tokens can be exchanged for privileges. Based on operant conditioning.
    - 精神病院里患者按时吃药和整理床铺就获得代币，攒够可以换零食或活动时间
 - Biofeedback
    - Using **electronic monitoring** to give individuals real-time information about physiological states (heart rate, muscle tension), enabling them to learn **self-regulation**. Used for stress management.
    - 通过屏幕实时看到自己的心率，练习用呼吸把心率降下来


 - Cognitive-behavioral therapies (CBT)
    - Integrates cognitive and behavioral approaches: **thoughts, behaviors, and emotions are interconnected**. Treats depression, anxiety, PTSD, OCD.
    - 同时改变消极想法和不良行为来改善情绪
 - Rational emotive behavior therapy (REBT)
    - Developed by <red>Albert Ellis</red>; identifies and restructures **irrational beliefs**. Considered a precursor to CBT.
    - 挑战我必须让所有人喜欢我这种非理性信念
 - Dialectical behavior therapy (DBT)
    - Combines **acceptance of self** with **changing unhealthy patterns**. Core skills: emotional regulation, distress tolerance, interpersonal effectiveness, mindfulness. Treats <red>BPD</red>, depression, anxiety, PTSD, eating disorders.
    - 接受自己当下的情绪，同时学习更健康的应对方式


 - Person-centered therapy
    - Provides a **supportive environment** for self-discovery and growth; focuses on the **present and future** rather than the past.
    - 治疗师不给建议，而是创造安全环境让来访者自己找到答案
 - Unconditional positive regard
    - The therapist offers **complete acceptance and support** regardless of what the client says or does.
    - 无论来访者说了什么，治疗师都不评判，给予完全的接纳
 - Active listening
    - The therapist **fully attends to, understands, responds to, and remembers** what the client expresses, validating their feelings.
    - 治疗师认真复述来访者的话，确认理解正确，让来访者感到被听见


 - Group therapy
    - Therapy conducted with multiple clients simultaneously. **Advantages**: shared experiences, peer support, reduced isolation, diverse perspectives. **Disadvantages**: less individual attention, discomfort sharing in front of others.
    - 几个有社交焦虑的人一起做团体治疗，互相分享经历和支持
 - Hypnosis
    - A state of **lowered conscious resistance** where the patient is more receptive to therapeutic suggestions; enhances access to the subconscious. **Effective** for pain and anxiety. <red>Not effective</red> for extracting accurate memories or age regression.
    - 用催眠帮助患者缓解慢性疼痛，但不能用来恢复准确的记忆


 - Psychoactive medication
    - Same as psychotropic medication — drugs that alter **brain chemistry** to treat mental disorders.
    - 通过调节神经递质来治疗心理障碍的药物
 - Antidepressants
    - Improve mood by affecting neurotransmitters, primarily <red>serotonin</red>. **SSRI** (Prozac, Zoloft, Lexapro): block serotonin reuptake, most common. **SNRI** (Effexor): serotonin + norepinephrine. **NDRI** (Wellbutrin): norepinephrine + dopamine.
    - 医生给抑郁症患者开了Prozac（一种SSRI），帮助提升血清素水平
 - Antianxiety drugs
    - Reduce sympathetic brain activity, promote relaxation. **Benzodiazepines** (Xanax, Valium): fast-acting but <red>addictive</red>. **Azapirones** (Buspar): non-sedating, lower dependency risk but slower onset.
    - 焦虑发作时服用Xanax可以快速镇静，但长期使用有成瘾风险
 - Lithium
    - A **mood stabilizer** commonly used to treat <red>bipolar disorder</red>; reduces the frequency and severity of manic and depressive episodes.
    - 双相障碍患者服用锂盐来稳定情绪波动
 - Antipsychotic medications
    - Eliminate positive symptoms of schizophrenia (hallucinations, delusions) by reducing **dopamine receptor activity**. **Typical/1st gen** (Haldol): effective for positive symptoms, high motor side-effect risk. **Atypical/2nd gen** (Risperdal, Zyprexa, Abilify): effective for both positive and negative symptoms, fewer motor side effects.
    - 精神分裂症患者服用抗精神病药后幻觉和妄想明显减少
 - Tardive dyskinesia
    - A potential side effect of long-term antipsychotic use: **involuntary, repetitive movements** of the face, jaw, and limbs, related to dopamine dysregulation.
    - 长期服用抗精神病药后出现无法控制的面部抽动和嘴巴咀嚼动作


 - Psychosurgery
    - Surgically altering brain tissue; a **last resort** when other treatments fail. Irreversible. Performed by psychiatrists and neurosurgeons collaboratively.
    - 在所有其他治疗都无效后，作为最后手段进行脑部手术
 - Lesioning
    - Making small, **targeted destructions** to specific brain areas.
    - 对特定脑区进行精确的小范围损毁来改变行为
 - Lobotomy
    - Severing connections to the **prefrontal cortex**; now largely <red>abandoned</red> due to severe side effects.
    - 早期切断前额叶连接的手术，导致患者变得像行尸走肉，现已基本废弃
 - Electroconvulsive therapy (ECT)
    - Sending small electrical currents through the brain to induce brief seizures; used for **severe, treatment-resistant depression**. May cause short-term memory loss.
    - 对药物无效的重度抑郁患者使用电休克治疗，可能导致短暂记忆丧失
 - TMS (transcranial magnetic stimulation)
    - **Non-invasive**; places a pulsed magnetic coil over the prefrontal area to stimulate nerve cells. Treats depression with minimal side effects.
    - 用磁场无创地刺激大脑前额叶区域来治疗抑郁，副作用比ECT小很多
